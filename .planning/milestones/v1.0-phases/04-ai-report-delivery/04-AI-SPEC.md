## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Hybrid — Content/Synthesis generation over pre-structured data (RPRT-01/02) + Structured Extraction extension (BRF soft signals, D-02)

**Description:**
A user-triggered synthesis step that combines four already-structured data sources for one property analysis — listing data (Phase 1), BRF financial health + A–F score (Phase 2), comparable sold-price comparison (Phase 3 PRICE-01), and SCB area demographics (Phase 3 AREA-01) — into one opinionated Swedish "vad du bör tänka på" report with a cross-source lead synthesis, themed sections (Ekonomi / Pris / Område), and red/green risk flags. A second, smaller AI operation extends the existing Phase 2 BRF extraction to capture structured, cited *soft signals* (planned stambyte, renovations, notable remarks) that feed the flags. Used by logged-in Swedish home buyers evaluating a specific listing. "Good" = every claim cites a real data point from the analysis (never generic advice), the report is opinionated but stops short of a buy/don't-buy verdict (stays inside "ej finansiell rådgivning"), flags are never invented by the model, and missing data sources are stated honestly rather than fabricated. Not an agent, RAG, or multi-agent system — two bounded LLM calls over the project's existing direct-Anthropic-SDK + Zod pipeline.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
<!-- The 3-5 behaviors that absolutely cannot go wrong in this system -->
1. **Fabricated data points** — the synthesis cites a number, comp, or flag that does not exist in the underlying structured data (violates D-06 "cite specific data" and the whole trust posture).
2. **Crossing into financial advice** — the report issues a buy/don't-buy verdict or recommendation, breaching the "ej finansiell rådgivning" legal line (D-04).
3. **Model-originated flags** — Claude raises a red/green flag the deterministic/structured layer did not produce, making flags non-reproducible and un-auditable (breaks D-01/D-03 hybrid contract; numeric flags MUST stay deterministic).
4. **Silent fabrication on partial data** — when BRF or market data is missing, the report invents content instead of stating the gap and its impact (D-07 partial-aware honesty).
5. **Soft-signal extraction error** — the extended BRF extraction reports a stambyte/renovation signal that isn't supported by the årsredovisning, or without its citation/confidence (carries Phase 2 D-10/D-11 risk into a new field set).

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** Swedish residential real estate — private bostadsrätt (co-op apartment) purchase due-diligence. Adjacent to consumer finance and mortgage lending, but deliberately positioned as *information / second opinion*, NOT regulated financial advice.

**User Population:** Private, non-expert Swedish home buyers evaluating a specific listing they are considering bidding on. They typically lack the skill to read a BRF årsredovisning, do not know the practitioner benchmarks (skuld/kvm bands, sparande per kvm, normal new-build debt), and are time-pressed in a fast bidding cycle. They are the same audience an Allabrf report or a knowledgeable buyer's-agent serves — but here they are alone with the data, which is the product's reason to exist ("the one thing their mäklare won't provide"). Login-gated (D-09); manual trigger (D-07).

**Stakes Level:** High. The decision the report informs is a 3–6 MSEK (Stockholm 4–6M+) leveraged purchase — among the largest financial commitments a Swedish household makes. The report does not execute or transact, and gives no verdict, so it is not Critical in the safety-of-life sense — but a materially wrong reading (e.g. calling a debt level "alarming" that is normal for new-build, or missing an imminent stambyte) can steer a real bid by tens of thousands to hundreds of thousands of SEK, and erodes the entire trust posture the product is built on.

**Output Consequence:** A buyer reads the report and adjusts their bidding behaviour — bids more confidently, bids lower to price in a flagged risk, walks away, or asks the mäklare/styrelse a specific follow-up question. Downstream the report is also saved/shared as a PDF (RPRT-03) and may be re-read days later or forwarded to a partner/parent co-signing the loan. There is no human reviewer between the AI output and the buyer's action — the report is consumed directly, unmediated.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

> What a knowledgeable Swedish buyer's-agent (köparmäklare), a BRF-savvy accountant/ekonom, or a bank mortgage advisor (bolånerådgivare) would call a GOOD vs. BAD assessment. In practitioner language; written to be measurable as eval rubric rows.

**Dimension: Correct reading of skuld/kvm against the right band**
Good (expert accepts): Reads föreningens skuld per kvm (total räntebärande lån / total yta) and places it in the right risk band — roughly <5 000 kr/kvm strong, ~5 000–12 000 medium, >12 000–15 000 a warning, >15 000 ohållbart — and states the number it used.
Bad (expert flags): Uses the wrong band (calls 6 000 kr/kvm "very high"), or reads *total* skuld in kronor as if it were skuld/kvm, or gives a verdict on debt without stating the per-kvm figure it is reasoning from.
Stakes: Critical
Source: SEB, SBAB, Borätt-köparskolan, banks' belåningsgrad benchmarks (search 1 & 2).

**Dimension: Contextualising debt for building age / new-build vs. old**
Good (expert accepts): Treats a 10 000–15 000 kr/kvm debt on a *nyproduktion* as normal (developer-loaded, not yet amortised) rather than alarming; conversely flags a high debt on an *old* building that has NOT recently done stambyte/tak/dränering as the more worrying case. Reads debt in light of byggår and recent major underhåll.
Bad (expert flags): Applies the old-building debt band to a new-build and raises a red flag on normal new-build leverage (or the inverse — waves through high debt on an un-renovated 1960s building).
Stakes: High
Source: SBAB / phmredovisning / ekonomifokus — "hög belåning behöver inte vara dåligt vid nyproduktion eller efter genomfört underhåll" (search 2).

**Dimension: Sustainability of the avgift (a low avgift is not automatically good)**
Good (expert accepts): Reads avgiftsnivå (kr/kvm/år) relative to the förening's debt, sparande and underhållsplan; explicitly recognises that a *suspiciously low* avgift can hide deferred maintenance or under-funded future stambyte (sparande well under the ~150–300 kr/kvm/år HSB schablon), and says so. Reads avgift trend over years where available.
Bad (expert flags): Calls a low avgift a positive in isolation ("attraktivt låg avgift") without checking debt/sparande/underhåll; or conflates monthly avgift with yearly avgift; or treats avgift as fixed and ignores höjningsrisk.
Stakes: High
Source: Boio, Stabelo, sparapengarna, HSB sparande-schablon 200–300 kr/kvm (median 2024 only ~164) (search 1 & 2).

**Dimension: Spotting an imminent / deferred stambyte and other large underhåll**
Good (expert accepts): Surfaces a planned or overdue stambyte (and tak/fasad/hiss/fönster) as a flag *with its cited source from the årsredovisning*, especially for buildings built before ~1970 where deferred stambyte is "a ticking financial bomb"; reflects it in the cost picture.
Bad (expert flags): Misses a stambyte that is stated in the underhållsplan/förvaltningsberättelse, OR invents/implies a stambyte that the document does not support, OR raises it without a citation a buyer could verify.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Svensk Fastighetsförmedling, Fastighetsbyrån, Boio — deferred stambyte pre-1970 (search 1); maps to D-02 cited soft signals.

**Dimension: Price premium read AGAINST the BRF's finances (the cross-source thread)**
Good (expert accepts): Connects the price comparison to the förening's economy in one judgement — e.g. "priset ligger 8 % över områdessnittet OCH föreningen är högt belånad → premien är svårare att motivera" — exactly the thing no single card says (D-05). Reads pris/kvm vs. area average with sample size in mind.
Bad (expert flags): Presents the sold-price comparison *as a valuation* of this specific apartment ("lägenheten är värd X"), or states the premium and the BRF debt as two disconnected facts, or treats a tiny comp sample as authoritative.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Practitioner cross-source synthesis; product's D-04/D-05/PROJECT.md "custom valuation is out of scope"; konsumenternas/FI advice boundary (search 3).

**Dimension: Honesty on partial data and uncertainty**
Good (expert accepts): When BRF or market data is missing, states clearly which source was absent and how that limits the assessment, and does not bid up confidence beyond what the data supports (carries Phase 2 D-10 confidence + D-07 partial-awareness).
Bad (expert flags): Fills a missing-data gap with generic filler or fabricated specifics; presents a partial assessment as complete; or hides low-confidence extracted figures behind confident prose.
Stakes: High
Source: Phase 3 D-08 (honest markers, never fabricate), Phase 2 D-10 confidence; SEB/Svensk Fast "be om 2–3 års årsredovisningar, läs underhållsplanen" — the gap is information, not advice.

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

> Domain-specific — how the generic risks actually manifest in a Swedish BRF second-opinion. These are the concrete things a köparmäklare / BRF-ekonom would catch.

1. **Unit / denominator confusion (monthly vs. yearly avgift; total skuld vs. skuld per kvm; total debt vs. räntebärande debt).** The single most common substantive error in BRF reading. A report that multiplies/divides the wrong denominator silently produces a 12× or area-scaled error and then reasons confidently from it. (Numeric flags are deterministic in code — D-01 — precisely to keep this out of the model; the synthesis must not "recompute" or restate a number in a different unit than the fact sheet provides.)
2. **Calling normal new-build leverage "alarming."** Flagging a 10 000–15 000 kr/kvm debt as a red flag on a nyproduktion (where it is expected) — or the inverse, waving through dangerous debt on an old un-renovated building. The band is right; the *context* (byggår, recent stambyte) is wrong.
3. **Presenting the sold-price comparison as a valuation.** Turning "pris/kvm ligger 8 % över områdessnittet (n sålda)" into an implied "rätt pris är X" / "övervärderad" verdict. This both crosses into the out-of-scope custom-valuation/liability line and over-claims what a small comp sample supports.
4. **"Låg avgift = bra" without sustainability check.** Praising a low monthly avgift in isolation, missing that it may be propped up at the expense of underhåll/sparande — the textbook trap practitioners warn about.
5. **Generic, non-citing filler ("läs årsredovisningen noga", "se över föreningens ekonomi").** Advice that says nothing specific about *this* listing's data. It reads as content but carries zero of the second-opinion value and violates D-06 (every claim cites a specific datum). A domain expert dismisses it as boilerplate.
6. **Drifting into a buy/don't-buy verdict or personalized recommendation.** "Detta är ett bra köp" / "du bör lägga max X" — opinionated is wanted (D-04), a verdict is not; it also pushes toward the regulated-advice line.
7. **Soft-signal hallucination/omission (stambyte, renovations, anmärkningar).** Reporting a stambyte/renovation the årsredovisning does not support, or dropping its citation/confidence, or *missing* one that is stated — the Phase 2 D-10/D-11 risk carried into the new D-02 field set.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

- **Not licensed financial advice — and deliberately staying on the safe side of that line.** Sweden's consumer financial-advice regime (lagen om finansiell rådgivning till konsumenter; Finansinspektionen authorization) governs advice about *placering av pengar / finansiella instrument*. A second opinion on a property purchase is not advice about a financial instrument, so no FI licence is required (PROJECT.md: "No license required for property analysis in Sweden"). The product nonetheless adopts the posture of the advice regime as a guardrail: it gives **no personalized buy/don't-buy or price recommendation** (D-04), so it cannot be construed as the kind of individualized financial råd that triggers adviser liability/damages. The recurring **"ej finansiell rådgivning" disclaimer** (D-09/D-12, on screen and in the PDF) makes this boundary explicit to the user.
- **Custom valuation explicitly out of scope (liability).** Per PROJECT.md and REQUIREMENTS.md, the product does NOT produce a Zestimate-style valuation — it lacks licensed transaction data (Mäklarstatistik/Lantmäteriet) and the liability that a stated "värde" carries. The price comparison is framed as *jämförelse mot sålda*, with an **"ej värdering"** disclaimer (Phase 3 D-09). Eval must treat any implied valuation as a compliance failure, not a quality nit.
- **Consumer-facing honesty / fair marketing.** As a consumer-facing tool, claims must not mislead (marknadsföringslagen spirit): source + freshness labels, confidence/"Osäker / Ej tillgänglig" markers, and honest partial-data statements (D-07/D-08/D-09/D-12) are the compliance surface here, not a specific sector regulation.
- **GDPR — lightly relevant.** The report synthesizes already-collected listing/BRF/market/area data; BRF årsredovisningar are public records. The only standing rule that bites the AI layer is operational: **never log the fact sheet** (financials) — log only `{ analysisId, code }` (already specified in §3/§4b). No special-category data; no new GDPR surface created by this phase.

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Direct Anthropic TypeScript SDK (`@anthropic-ai/sdk`) + Zod v4 (`zod/v4`) — **no orchestration framework**

**Version:** `@anthropic-ai/sdk` ^0.102.0 (in use), `zod` ^4.3.6 (in use)

**Rationale:**
Phase 4's AI work is a strict subset of the pattern Phase 2 already runs on the bare Anthropic SDK: (a) ONE synthesis call combining already-structured data into an opinionated Swedish report, and (b) EXTENDING the existing `brfExtractionSchema` / `extract.ts` with new cited soft-signal fields. It is explicitly **not** an agent, RAG, or multi-agent problem, so any orchestration framework would add abstraction with zero payoff (matrix Anti-Patterns #1 and #7). The stack is TypeScript-only (Next.js 16, no Python), Claude-only by design with model flexibility explicitly not needed, and Zod v4 is already the project's structured-output contract — the SDK's structured-output helper (`zodOutputFormat`, already used in `src/lib/brf/extract.ts`) gives schema-constrained synthesis output, and the extended extraction reuses the same parse / citation / confidence pipeline, the `cost.ts` cost guard, and the Promptfoo + Vitest eval discipline. Maximum consistency, minimum new surface for a 5–10 h/week solo dev.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| LangChain.js (`@langchain/anthropic`) `withStructuredOutput` + Zod | The only TS, structured-output-capable escape hatch — but only justified if synthesis later becomes genuinely multi-step (plan→draft→critique) or needs provider-swapping. Neither is in Phase 4 scope; adds an abstraction layer over the SDK we already use. Revisit-only-if. |
| LangGraph | Stateful graph overkill for one linear synthesis call (Anti-Pattern #7). No state machine, branching, or human-in-the-loop needed. |
| Claude Agent SDK | Autonomous tool-use / MCP loops — unused weight for two bounded calls (same logic that ruled it out in Phase 2). |
| LlamaIndex | RAG/retrieval framework — there is no retrieval step; all data is already structured and in hand. |
| CrewAI / AutoGen / Google ADK / Haystack | Python/Java only — contradict the TypeScript-only stack. |
| OpenAI Agents SDK | Wrong vendor — project is Claude-only by design. |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Yes (Claude-only) — a conscious, pre-existing project decision (PROJECT.md: "Claude for analysis"). Model flexibility is explicitly not needed; the trade-off buys integration simplicity and consistency with Phases 1–3.

### Entry Point Pattern

The synthesis call is **strictly simpler than the Phase 2 extraction**: no PDF document block, no Files API, no base64 — the input is a few-thousand-token JSON-stringified bundle of already-structured data. One Sonnet call, schema-validated structured output, same coded-error + cost-guard discipline.

```ts
// src/lib/report/synthesize.ts  (NEW — mirrors src/lib/brf/extract.ts conventions)
import Anthropic from "@anthropic-ai/sdk";
import { zodOutputFormat } from "@anthropic-ai/sdk/helpers/zod";
import { reportSchema, type AiReport } from "@/lib/schemas/report";
import { REPORT_SYNTHESIS_SYSTEM_PROMPT } from "@/lib/report/prompt";
import { type ClaudeUsage } from "@/lib/brf/cost";

// Module-scope, server-only client — instantiated ONLY in this file (T-02-09 pattern).
// Reads ANTHROPIC_API_KEY from the env; never configured for browser use.
const client = new Anthropic();

// Synthesis is opinionated judgement, not extraction → the more capable tier (D-discretion).
const MODEL = "claude-sonnet-4-6";

export interface SynthesizeInput {
  // Pre-assembled, already-structured facts. NOT raw PDFs — the deterministic
  // numeric flags and the cited soft signals are computed/extracted upstream;
  // this call only NARRATES and PRIORITIZES them (D-03), never originates them.
  factSheet: string;     // JSON.stringify of the listing+brf+price+area+flags bundle
  analysisId: string;    // logged for traceability — never log the factSheet contents
}

export interface SynthesizeResult {
  parsed: AiReport;
  usage: ClaudeUsage;
}

export async function synthesizeReport(
  input: SynthesizeInput,
): Promise<SynthesizeResult> {
  const { factSheet, analysisId } = input;
  try {
    const runOnce = () =>
      client.messages.parse({
        model: MODEL,
        max_tokens: 4096,          // bounded — never unbounded in production (§4b.3)
        temperature: 0.4,          // low; opinionated voice, but reproducible-ish
        system: REPORT_SYNTHESIS_SYSTEM_PROMPT,  // stable prefix → cache-eligible
        messages: [
          {
            role: "user",
            content: [
              {
                type: "text",
                text: factSheet,                     // the structured bundle
                cache_control: { type: "ephemeral" }, // cache the larger fact block
              },
              { type: "text", text: "Skriv rapporten enligt schemat." },
            ],
          },
        ],
        // CURRENT shape — output_config.format, NOT the deprecated top-level
        // output_format (claude-api skill). Matches extract.ts exactly.
        output_config: { format: zodOutputFormat(reportSchema) },
      });

    let message = await runOnce();

    if (message.stop_reason === "refusal") throw new Error("CLAUDE_REFUSAL");
    if (message.stop_reason === "max_tokens") {
      message = await runOnce();              // retry once, then give up
      if (message.stop_reason === "max_tokens") throw new Error("CLAUDE_MAX_TOKENS");
    }
    if (!message.parsed_output) throw new Error("CLAUDE_PARSE_EMPTY");

    return {
      parsed: message.parsed_output,
      usage: {
        input_tokens: message.usage.input_tokens,
        output_tokens: message.usage.output_tokens,
        cache_creation_input_tokens: message.usage.cache_creation_input_tokens ?? 0,
        cache_read_input_tokens: message.usage.cache_read_input_tokens ?? 0,
      },
    };
  } catch (error) {
    // Log ONLY the analysis id + a stable code — never the factSheet (financials).
    const code = error instanceof Error ? error.message : "CLAUDE_CALL_FAILED";
    console.error("[report-synthesize]", { analysisId, code });
    throw new Error(code, { cause: error });
  }
}
```

> NOTE on `parse` vs `beta.messages.parse`: `extract.ts` uses `client.beta.messages.parse` because it also needs the **Files API** beta header for large PDFs. Synthesis has no document/Files-API need, so plain `client.messages.parse` is sufficient. If you prefer one code path, `client.beta.messages.parse` (no `betas` array) also works — verify the chosen method against the installed types before committing.

### Key Abstractions

| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| `client.messages.parse({ output_config: { format } })` | The structured-output entry point. Validates Claude's JSON against a schema server-side and returns a typed `parsed_output`. `output_config.format` is current; top-level `output_format` is deprecated. | The single synthesis call — guarantees the report comes back in `reportSchema` shape (RPRT-01). |
| `zodOutputFormat(schema)` | Wraps a Zod v4 schema into the SDK's `AutoParseableOutputFormat`. The schema's `.describe()` text is emitted into the JSON schema handed to Claude and steers generation. | Wrapping `reportSchema` and the extended `brfExtractionSchema`. Same helper Phase 2 already uses. |
| `cache_control: { type: "ephemeral" }` | Marks a content block as a prompt-cache breakpoint (5-min TTL). Prefix-match: stable content first, volatile last (cached at $0.10/MTok vs $1–3/MTok cold). | Cache the large fact block / stable system prompt so a regenerate (D-08) or `max_tokens` retry is cheap. Min cacheable prefix ~1024 tokens. |
| `message.stop_reason` | Why generation stopped: `"end_turn"`, `"max_tokens"`, `"refusal"`, etc. `"refusal"` is a guardrail trip. | Branch BEFORE reading `parsed_output`: refusal → surface, never retry; `max_tokens` → retry once. Identical to `extract.ts`. |
| `message.usage` → `costSek(usage)` | Per-call token counts (incl. cache fields) → the reused SEK cost function. | Enforce the per-report cost guard against the inherited <5 SEK/analysis budget (§4b.5). |

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Letting Claude originate flags or issue a verdict.** The single largest failure mode for THIS system (FM3/FM2). The model must only *narrate and prioritize* flags that the deterministic code (numeric) and the cited extraction (soft signals) already produced — and must stop short of buy/don't-buy (D-04). Mitigation: the fact sheet contains the pre-computed flags; the system prompt forbids inventing flags or recommending; `reportSchema` has **no** `verdict`/`recommendation`/`new_flag` field to populate (shape the schema to make the failure unrepresentable, the same trick Phase 2 used to keep `betyg` out of the extraction schema).
2. **`citations: { enabled: true }` + structured outputs = 400.** The two are mutually exclusive (documented in `extract.ts:157`). For synthesis you do NOT want API-level citations anyway — D-06 "cite specific data" means the report references data points that are *already in the fact sheet*, carried as schema fields (e.g. a `sourceRef` string per claim), not as document-citation blocks. Do not try to enable both.
3. **Structured outputs force every key present — `null`, never missing.** A Zod field that may be absent must be `.nullable()`, NEVER `.optional()` (see `extractedField` in `schemas/brf.ts`). On partial data (D-07: missing BRF or market source) the report must carry an explicit "ej tillgänglig" marker field set to a value, not omit a section — silent omission reads as fabrication-by-absence (FM4).
4. **Silent prompt-cache invalidation.** Any byte change in the prefix invalidates the cache. If the system prompt or the leading fact-sheet block contains a timestamp, a per-request id, or unsorted JSON keys, `usage.cache_read_input_tokens` silently stays 0 and every regenerate pays full price. Keep volatile content (the per-request instruction) AFTER the last `cache_control` breakpoint; serialize the fact sheet with stable key order.
5. **Forgetting the prompt-version bump on BOTH prompts.** This phase changes two prompts: the NEW `REPORT_SYNTHESIS_PROMPT_VERSION` and the BUMPED `BRF_EXTRACTION_PROMPT_VERSION` (extended for soft signals, D-02). Each change MUST bump its version tag and re-run its promptfoo eval set (Phase 2 discipline) — the extraction change additionally re-runs the full Phase 2 eval because it touches a cross-phase schema/migration.

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**

Two calls, two tiers — confirmed against the `claude-api` skill model table (cached 2026-04-15):

|------|-------|----|--------------|--------|

Sonnet is the right rung. | `max_tokens: 4096`, `temperature: 0.4`, 200K+ context (input is ~2–4k tokens, never near the limit) |
| **Extended extraction** (D-02 soft signals) | Claude Haiku 4.5 | `claude-haiku-4-5` | UNCHANGED from Phase 2 — the soft-signal fields ride the *existing* `extract.ts` Haiku call; do not add a second model or a second pass. | `max_tokens: 2048` (raise only if the added fields truncate output — watch `stop_reason`), `temperature: 0` (deterministic extraction) |

Notes that bit if ignored:
- Use the **bare** model IDs above (`claude-sonnet-4-6`, `claude-haiku-4-5`) — the skill warns: do NOT append date suffixes. (The installed `extract.ts` currently pins `claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`; the dated form still resolves, but new code should use the bare ID.)
- `temperature` is valid on **Sonnet 4.6** (sampling params are only removed on Opus 4.7). Keep it low (`0.4`) — the report must be reproducible enough that the same data yields a substantially similar report, satisfying the determinism spirit of the trust posture.
- Do NOT enable `thinking`/`effort` for synthesis — this is a single structured generation, not an agentic loop; extended thinking adds cost and latency with no quality payoff here. `effort` would also 400 on Haiku 4.5 for the extraction call.

**Core Pattern:**

`assemble → one call → schema-validated structured report`, run inside a Next.js server action that mirrors `src/actions/analyze-brf.ts`:

1. **Deterministic-first.** Numeric flags (skuld/kvm band, avgift level, price ±% vs area snitt) are computed in pure TS in `src/lib/report/flags.ts` — reproducible, listable on a "Så flaggar vi" page (D-01a). The model never computes these.
2. **Assemble the fact sheet** (`fact-sheet.ts`): combine the four `safeParse`-validated sources + the deterministic flags + the cited soft signals into ONE stable-key-order JSON string. Mark each missing source explicitly (`brf: { status: "ej_tillgänglig" }`) so D-07 partial-awareness is encoded in the input, not left for the model to infer.
3. **One `synthesizeReport` call** (Section 3 entry point) → typed `AiReport`.
4. **Cost guard** (reused `costSek`): compute SEK from `result.usage`; if it exceeds the inherited <5 SEK/analysis budget, log and flag — never silently overspend.
5. **Persist** the validated report to a new `analyses` jsonb column + a `report_status`/staleness fingerprint (D-08), independently of the existing brf/market columns.

```ts
// src/actions/generate-report.ts  (mirrors analyze-brf.ts: auth → compute → persist)
"use server";
export async function generateReport(analysisId: string) {
  // 1. auth + ownership (login-only, D-09) — same guard as analyze-brf.ts
  // 2. load + safeParse the four sources; compute deterministic flags (flags.ts)
  const factSheet = assembleFactSheet({ listing, brf, price, area, flags, softSignals });
  // 3. single synthesis call
  const { parsed, usage } = await synthesizeReport({ factSheet, analysisId });
  // 4. cost guard — reuse Phase 2 pattern
  const sek = costSek(usage);
  if (sek > 5) console.warn("[report-cost]", { analysisId, sek }); // budget breach signal
  // 5. validate-on-write + persist with a data fingerprint for D-08 staleness
  const data = reportSchema.parse(parsed);
  await persistReport(analysisId, { data, usage, sek, dataFingerprint });
}
```

**Tool Use:**

None. This system uses **no tool calling, no function calling, no MCP, no agent loop**. Structured output via `output_config.format` is the *only* API feature beyond plain messages. The deterministic flags are ordinary TypeScript, not Claude tools. (Confirmed: the `claude-api` skill decision tree puts "extraction / Q&A / synthesis" at the single-LLM-call tier — agents are explicitly not warranted.)

**State Management:**

- **No conversational state, no memory, no session.** Each report is a single stateless call; the entire context is the fact sheet assembled per invocation.
- **Persistence:** the report is stored as a jsonb column on the `analyses` row (RLS-scoped to the owner), alongside a `report_status` and a **data fingerprint** (hash of the inputs the report was generated from). D-08 staleness = "current input fingerprint ≠ stored fingerprint" → show the "uppdatera" marker; regenerate is a fresh stateless call, never a silent auto-refire.
- **Read-path guard:** `safeParseReportData` on the analysis page (mirroring `safeParseBrfData`) — a malformed/drifted stored report degrades to "not generated yet", never a white screen (CR-01 pattern).

**Context Window Strategy:**

Effectively a non-problem here, by design — and that is the point worth stating. The input is a **small, pre-structured fact sheet (~2–4k tokens)**, not a 20 MB årsredovisning. Sonnet 4.6's 200K+ window dwarfs it; there is no chunking, no RAG, no summarisation, no truncation. The only context discipline that matters is **prompt-cache hygiene** (stable prefix so regenerates are cheap, §4b.4) and **bounding the OUTPUT** with `max_tokens: 4096` so a runaway report can't blow latency or cost. If the assembled fact sheet ever approaches the window (it won't, given the four bounded sources), that is a signal the deterministic pre-aggregation in `fact-sheet.ts` is leaking raw data it should be summarising upstream — fix the assembly, do not lean on the model's context.

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice.
> **This project uses Zod v4 (`zod/v4`), NOT Pydantic** — it is a TypeScript/Next.js stack. The structured-output section below is the Zod equivalent.

### Structured Outputs with Zod (the project's "Pydantic")

The output contract is a Zod v4 schema, wrapped with `zodOutputFormat` and passed to `messages.parse` — the SDK validates Claude's JSON server-side and returns a typed `parsed_output`. Same mechanism as `brfExtractionSchema` in `src/lib/schemas/brf.ts`; the report just has a different shape.

**The report schema** — shaped to make the critical failure modes *unrepresentable*. There is no `verdict`/`recommendation` field (D-04, FM2) and no free-form `flag` the model can mint (D-03, FM3 — flags arrive pre-computed in the fact sheet; the model only references their ids). Every section field is `.nullable()`, never `.optional()`, so partial data (D-07) yields an explicit `null` the UI renders as "Ej tillgänglig", not a silently missing key (FM4):

```ts
// src/lib/schemas/report.ts
import { z } from "zod/v4";

// A single opinionated claim that MUST point back to a real datum (D-06).
const citedClaim = z.object({
  text: z.string().describe("Påståendet, på svenska. Tolkning — inte upprepning av rådata."),
  sourceRef: z
    .string()
    .describe("Vilken datapunkt påståendet bygger på, t.ex. 'brf.skuldPerKvm' eller 'flag:price_above_area'. MÅSTE finnas i faktaunderlaget."),
});

const themedSection = z.object({
  // 'ej_tillgänglig' lets the model state a gap honestly instead of fabricating (D-07/FM4).
  status: z.enum(["bedömd", "ej_tillgänglig"]),
  claims: z.array(citedClaim).describe("Tolkningar för temat. Tom om ej_tillgänglig."),
});

export const reportSchema = z.object({
  // The cross-source thread no single card can state (D-05). 1–2 sentences.
  leadSynthesis: z.string().describe(
    "Tvärgående syntes som kopplar ihop pris/BRF/område. Ej köp/sälj-råd (D-04).",
  ),
  ekonomi: themedSection,
  pris: themedSection,
  omrade: themedSection,
  // Flags the model chose to SURFACE/PRIORITIZE — by id only, from the fact sheet.
  // The model can reorder and contextualise; it can NEVER add an id not present upstream.
  prioritizedFlagIds: z
    .array(z.string())
    .describe("Id:n för flaggor som redan finns i faktaunderlaget, prioriterade. Hitta ALDRIG på nya."),
  // NO verdict / recommendation / buy-signal field — the line is unrepresentable (D-04/FM2).
});
export type AiReport = z.infer<typeof reportSchema>;
```

**The extended soft-signal fields** reuse the exact `extractedField` factory from `schemas/brf.ts` — each soft signal carries the same `{ value, confidence, sourceQuote, pageRef }` shape, so the citation (D-11) and confidence (D-10) trust pipeline extends for free (FM5). Added to `brfExtractionSchema`:

// src/lib/schemas/brf.ts — EXTENDS the existing brfExtractionSchema (D-02).

stambytePlanerat: extractedField(
  z.enum(["planerat", "nyligen_genomfort", "ej_nämnt"]),

storreRenoveringar: extractedField(z.string()).describe(
  "Planerade/genomförda större renoveringar (tak, fasad, hiss, fönster), ordagrant citat",

  "Övriga noterbara anmärkningar i förvaltningsberättelsen/revisionsberättelsen",

**Retry / validation discipline** (identical posture to `extract.ts`, do NOT reinvent):
- `messages.parse` already validates against the schema — a shape violation surfaces as a parse failure, not silent garbage. There is no separate "ask the model to fix its JSON" loop.
- Branch on `stop_reason` BEFORE reading `parsed_output`: `"refusal"` → throw `CLAUDE_REFUSAL`, **no retry** (a guardrail trip won't fix on re-ask); `"max_tokens"` → retry **exactly once**, then throw `CLAUDE_MAX_TOKENS`; empty `parsed_output` → `CLAUDE_PARSE_EMPTY`. Bounded retries only — never loop.
- **Log only** `{ analysisId, code }` server-side — never the fact sheet (financials/GDPR). Surface a Swedish user message at the action layer, not in `synthesize.ts`.
- **safeParse on RENDER too:** the persisted report is re-validated with `safeParseReportData` on the analysis page (mirroring `safeParseBrfData`). Stored-JSON drift → "not generated yet" affordance, never a crash (CR-01).

### Async-First Design

- **Server actions are already `async`** — `synthesizeReport` returns a `Promise`; `await` it inside the `"use server"` action (`generate-report.ts`). Next.js owns the event loop; there is no manual loop to manage and **no `asyncio.run()`-style mistake to make** (that is a Python pitfall — the TS equivalent would be blocking the request, which `await` avoids).
- **`await`, do NOT stream, for synthesis.** Structured-output validation (`parsed_output`) requires the *complete* message — you cannot Zod-validate a half-streamed JSON object. The report is also user-triggered behind a button (D-07), not a live chat, so token-by-token streaming buys no UX here; a single awaited call with a loading state ("Genererar rapport…") is the right shape. (If a future long-output variant is added, the SDK's `.stream()` + `.finalMessage()` would be the path — but that is not Phase 4.)
- **The one real async mistake to avoid:** firing `generateReport` without debouncing the button, allowing concurrent duplicate Sonnet calls on a double-click → 2x cost. Guard with a `report_status: "generating"` lock (optimistic UI + a server-side in-flight check), the same way a regenerate (D-08) must not stack on an in-flight run.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

- **System vs. user split:** the *role, rules, and the hard prohibitions* live in the **system** prompt (`REPORT_SYNTHESIS_SYSTEM_PROMPT`) — "du är en svensk bostadsrådgivare; cite varje påstående; aldrig köp/sälj-råd (D-04); hitta aldrig på flaggor (D-03)". The **user** message carries only the per-request fact sheet + a terse instruction. This split keeps the system prefix byte-stable (cacheable) while the fact sheet varies — and it is the right separation for the cache-control placement in §4b.4.
- **Few-shot: inline, not retrieved.** Like the BRF extraction prompt's inline denominator examples, give the synthesis prompt 1–2 inline examples of a *good* lead synthesis (cross-source, cited, no verdict) vs. a *bad* one (generic advice, or a buy recommendation) to lock the D-04/D-06 line. No dynamic retrieval — there is no corpus to retrieve from.
- **`max_tokens` always explicit:** `4096` for synthesis (a few themed sections + lead), `2048` for the extended extraction (watch `stop_reason: "max_tokens"` after adding the three soft-signal fields — raise only if it actually truncates). **Never leave `max_tokens` unbounded in production** — an unbounded report is a latency and cost hazard.
- **Versioned prompts + promptfoo regression — applies to BOTH prompts.** Every change to `REPORT_SYNTHESIS_SYSTEM_PROMPT` bumps `REPORT_SYNTHESIS_PROMPT_VERSION` and re-runs its eval set; every change to the soft-signal extraction bumps `BRF_EXTRACTION_PROMPT_VERSION` and re-runs the **full Phase 2 eval** (it is a cross-phase schema change). A regression must be attributable to a specific prompt revision — this is the Phase 2 discipline, non-negotiable here.

### Context Window Management

This is a **synthesis-over-structured-data** system, so the RAG/conversation/agent strategies do not apply — the relevant discipline is *input assembly*, not context fitting:
- **Input is small and bounded** (~2–4k tokens of pre-structured JSON) vs. Sonnet's 200K+ window. No chunking, no reranking, no truncation, no summarisation-in-the-loop. If you find yourself managing context here, the fact-sheet assembly is over-stuffing raw data — pre-aggregate in `fact-sheet.ts` instead.
- **Output is the thing to bound,** via `max_tokens` (above), so themed sections stay concise interpretations (D-05) rather than restating every raw number already on screen.
- **Prompt-cache as the cost lever:** put the stable system prompt and the (stable-key-ordered) fact-sheet block before the `cache_control` breakpoint; the volatile per-request instruction goes last. A regenerate (D-08) or a `max_tokens` retry then reads cached tokens at $0.10/MTok instead of re-paying $3/MTok. Verify with `usage.cache_read_input_tokens > 0`; if it is 0 across regenerates, a timestamp or unsorted key is silently invalidating the prefix.

### Cost and Latency Budget

**Per-report estimate (synthesis, Sonnet 4.6 @ $3 in / $15 out per MTok):**
- Input: system prompt + fact sheet ≈ 3,000 tokens → 3,000 / 1e6 × $3 = **$0.009**
- Output: lead + three themed sections ≈ 1,200 tokens → 1,200 / 1e6 × $15 = **$0.018**
- ≈ **$0.027 ≈ 0.30 SEK** per report (at `USD_SEK_RATE = 11`) — comfortably inside the inherited **<5 SEK/analysis** guard. Even with a `max_tokens` retry and zero cache benefit, a report lands well under 1 SEK. The extended soft-signal extraction adds only a few hundred Haiku output tokens to the *existing* Phase 2 call — a rounding error on its already-budgeted cost.
- **Reuse `costSek` from `src/lib/brf/cost.ts` unchanged** to compute the real figure from `result.usage`, and apply the same `> 5 SEK` warn/flag guard in `generate-report.ts`. (Latency: a single Sonnet 4.6 call at this size is typically a few seconds — well within a button-triggered "Genererar…" spinner; D-07 manual trigger means there is no per-pageview cost.)

**Caching strategy:**
- **Exact-match / regenerate caching:** the prompt cache (above) makes the D-08 regenerate cheap. Beyond that, do not auto-regenerate — D-07/D-08 already gate the expensive call behind a manual button + a staleness fingerprint, which is the strongest cost control (no semantic cache needed for a one-shot, user-triggered, login-gated call).
- **No semantic cache** — each report is keyed to one analysis's unique data; cross-analysis cache hits don't exist.

**Cheaper-model routing (already in place):** the cost-bearing split is exactly the §4 model tiering — **Haiku 4.5** for the mechanical extraction (incl. the new soft signals), **Sonnet 4.6** only for the one judgement-heavy synthesis. The deterministic numeric flags run as **pure TypeScript (zero LLM cost)** — the cheapest possible "model" for the classification sub-task, and the reason flags are reproducible and auditable (D-01a).

## 5. Evaluation Strategy

> The system is a **synthesis-over-structured-data** generator (RPRT-01/02) plus a small **extraction extension** (D-02 soft signals). The dominant eval concern is therefore NOT generative fluency — it is **fidelity to a closed input**: the synthesis must narrate-and-prioritize what the fact sheet already contains and never originate a number, a flag, or a verdict. The single most important reframing (from §1b FM1 and the domain researcher) is that **we do not test the model's arithmetic — the numbers are pre-computed in code (D-01).** Instead we test that the prose does not *re-derive or restate a number in a different unit/denominator* than the fact sheet supplied. The hardest failure to catch — generic, non-citing filler (FM5 / domain FM5) — is the one that most needs an LLM judge; everything verdict- or fabrication-shaped is defended deterministically by the schema first.
>
> Evals split into three layers that match the §3/§4 architecture, deliberately mirroring the Phase 2 split:
> - **Synthesis evals** (non-deterministic, Sonnet) — run the labelled report reference set; the unit of truth is the `AiReport` object (`leadSynthesis`, the three `themedSection`s, `prioritizedFlagIds`). Promptfoo for prompt-regression + LLM-judge rows; Vitest for the deterministic structural assertions.
> - **Extended-extraction evals** (non-deterministic, Haiku) — the three NEW soft-signal fields (`stambytePlanerat`, `storreRenoveringar`, `ovrigaAnmarkningar`) ride the **existing Phase 2 `evals/extractor.eval.ts` + `promptfooconfig.yaml` harness**; a soft-signal change bumps `BRF_EXTRACTION_PROMPT_VERSION` and re-runs the **full Phase 2 eval set** (cross-phase schema/migration change — §4b discipline, non-negotiable).
> - **Deterministic-layer evals** (pure TS, zero LLM) — `flags.ts` (numeric flag derivation, D-01a) and `safeParseReportData` (read-path guard, CR-01) are ordinary Vitest golden/property tests. Given a known fact sheet, the flag set is asserted exactly. These are not "AI evals" but they are where FM3 (model-originated flags) and FM1 (unit confusion) are *most cheaply* defended — by making the model unable to touch the numbers at all.

FAIL: states the premium and the BRF debt as two disconnected facts; applies the wrong debt band for building age; presents the comp as a valuation; or treats a tiny comp sample as authoritative. | **LLM judge** (rubric scored against the §1b dimensions; calibrated) **+ Human** (buyer's-agent: "is this the thread you'd actually tell a client?"). | **High** |
| **Partial-data honesty** (§1 FM4, D-07) | PASS: when the fact sheet marks a source absent (`brf: { status: "ej_tillgänglig" }` / no market data), the matching `themedSection.status` is `ej_tillgänglig`, the report **states which source was missing and how it limits the assessment**, and does not bid up confidence or invent specifics for the gap. FAIL: a missing source is filled with generic filler or fabricated detail; a partial assessment is presented as complete; or low-confidence extracted figures are hidden behind confident prose. | **Code** (assert section `status` matches the input's source-presence flags) **+ LLM judge** (is the gap stated honestly, or papered over?) over the two partial-data reference cases. | **High** |
| **No generic, non-citing filler** (§1b FM5, the "boilerplate" trap) | PASS: every claim says something specific about *this* listing's data ("föreningens skuld/kvm är 11 200 → i varningsbandet"); no "läs årsredovisningen noga" / "se över föreningens ekonomi" boilerplate that would survive copy-pasting to any other listing. FAIL: a claim that carries zero second-opinion value — generic advice a domain expert dismisses as filler. | **LLM judge** ("would this exact sentence be true of a different listing? if yes → filler", calibrated against the experienced-buyer stand-in) **+ Human** spot-check. | **High** |
| **Soft-signal extraction accuracy + citation/confidence** (§1 FM5, D-02/D-10/D-11) | PASS: `stambytePlanerat` / `storreRenoveringar` / `ovrigaAnmarkningar` each carry a `sourceQuote` that appears verbatim in the cited `pageRef` and actually supports the signal; a stambyte stated in the underhållsplan/förvaltningsberättelse is captured; an unsupported one is NOT invented; confidence is low where the text is ambiguous. FAIL: a soft signal with no/wrong citation, an invented stambyte/renovation, or a clearly-stated one missed. | **Code** (substring + page-bound citation check, reuses the Phase 2 citation validator) **+ LLM judge** (does the quote support the signal? calibrated) **+ Human** (BRF-ekonom) for the buried-text edge cases. Runs inside the **Phase 2** harness. | **Critical** |
| **Per-report cost adherence** (§1 cost guard, §4b budget) | PASS: per-report Sonnet cost < 5 SEK computed from `message.usage` via `costSek`; a regenerate/`max_tokens` retry reads cached tokens (`cache_read_input_tokens` > 0); synthesis uses `claude-sonnet-4-6`, extraction stays on `claude-haiku-4-5`. FAIL: any report ≥ 5 SEK, the wrong model id, or cache misses on every regenerate (timestamp/unsorted-key invalidation, §4b.4). | **Code** — assert computed SEK and model id over the reference run; assert `cache_read_input_tokens > 0` on a back-to-back regenerate fixture. | **High** |
| **Refusal / truncation handling** (§3 entry point, §4b retry) | PASS: `stop_reason: "refusal"` throws `CLAUDE_REFUSAL` with **no retry**; `max_tokens` retries **exactly once** then throws `CLAUDE_MAX_TOKENS`; empty `parsed_output` → `CLAUDE_PARSE_EMPTY`; none surface partial/silent data; the action layer shows a Swedish message and never logs the fact sheet. FAIL: a refusal retried (wasted budget), truncated JSON surfaced as a real report, or the fact sheet appearing in a log line. | **Code** — simulated stop-reason fixtures + a log-redaction assertion (`{ analysisId, code }` only). | **Medium** |

### Eval Tooling

**Primary Tool:** **Vitest + Promptfoo — both already installed and wired in Phase 2** (`promptfoo@^0.120.19`, `vitest@^4.1.8`, the `evals/` dir, `npm run test`, `npm run eval`). Vitest carries the deterministic structural/contract assertions (schema, no-verdict invariant, sourceRef/flag-id resolution, numeral-binding, cost, stop-reason); Promptfoo carries the prompt-regression + LLM-judge rows (verdict line, filler, synthesis quality). **This is a deliberate continuation of the Phase 2 stack — no new eval dependency is added.**

> **Why NOT RAGAS / Arize Phoenix (template defaults overridden — same rationale as Phase 2 §5):**
> - **RAGAS is Python + RAG-specific.** This stack is TypeScript-only, and there is **no retrieval step** — the input is a small pre-structured fact sheet, not retrieved documents. RAGAS faithfulness/context-precision metrics have no target here. The *concept* of faithfulness still matters and is captured directly by the "no fabricated data point" + "no unit re-derivation" Vitest/Promptfoo rows (a set-membership check against the closed fact sheet — strictly stronger than a similarity-based faithfulness score for this closed-input case).
> - **Arize Phoenix is overkill for v1.** It is an OpenTelemetry tracing platform aimed at multi-step RAG/agent pipelines. This phase is **two bounded LLM calls** behind a manual button, login-gated, on a Vercel/Next serverless solo-dev budget (<$100/mo) with **no tracing infra today**. Standing up Phoenix (a service to host, instrument, and maintain) is disproportionate. The right-sized minimum is **already in place**: structured server-side logging of `{ analysisId, code, model, usage, sek, promptVersion }` via the existing `cost.ts`/`costSek` path, surfaced through the project's existing **PostHog** + **Supabase** — exactly the §7 posture Phase 2 chose. Adopt a tracing platform (Langfuse self-hosted) only if a later phase makes synthesis genuinely multi-step. **Recorded explicitly: full tracing infra is NOT warranted for Phase 4 v1.**

### Reference Dataset

**Size:** **10–20 fact-sheet → report examples** to start (per ai-evals "10–20 high-quality examples"). Begin at ~12 and grow toward 20 as production failures surface; expand from real misses, not hypothetical coverage. The soft-signal extraction reuses and lightly extends the existing **Phase 2 12–20 årsredovisning** reference set (the same PDFs now also carry expected stambyte/renovation/anmärkning labels).

**Composition** (deliberately spans the §1/§1b failure surface — each fixture is a complete assembled fact sheet plus its expected `AiReport`):
- **Clean strong BRF (~2):** low skuld/kvm, healthy sparande, no major underhåll — the happy path; report should be confidently positive without crossing into "bra köp".
- **High-debt old building (~2):** skuld/kvm in the warning/ohållbart band on a pre-1970 building with NO recent stambyte — the genuinely worrying case the report must flag (and connect to cost picture), with the deferred-stambyte soft signal present.
- **New-build debt-inversion (~2):** 10 000–15 000 kr/kvm on a nyproduktion — must be read as *normal/expected*, NOT alarming (domain FM2). The single most important "context over band" case.
- **Partial — no BRF uploaded (~1):** `brf` source absent → `ekonomi.status` must be `ej_tillgänglig`, gap stated honestly, no fabricated finances (FM4).
- **Partial — no market data (~1):** price/area absent → `pris`/`omrade` sections `ej_tillgänglig`, no invented comp (FM4).
- **Price-premium-vs-weak-finances (~2):** the flagship cross-source case (D-05) — pris/kvm above area snitt AND high BRF debt; lead synthesis must connect them and stop short of "övervärderad"/valuation (domain FM3).
- **Unit-trap cases (~2):** fact sheets where a monthly *and* annual avgift, or total debt *and* skuld/kvm, are both present — the report must quote the right pre-computed figure in the right unit and never restate it converted (domain FM1).
- **Generic-filler adversarial (~1):** a thin fact sheet that tempts boilerplate — the report must still be specific or honestly say little, never pad with "läs årsredovisningen" filler (FM5).
- **Verdict-bait (~1):** a strong-looking listing where a naive model would say "detta är ett bra köp / lägg max X" — must stay opinionated-but-no-verdict (FM2).

**Labeling:** **Domain-expert ground truth, per the §1b roles — not engineer-guessed.**
- A **buyer's-agent / köparmäklare** calibrates the lead-synthesis rubric (D-05) and labels the **verdict line** (is this opinionated-but-not-advice?) and whether the price-vs-BRF reasoning is the thread they'd actually tell a client.
- A **BRF-savvy accountant / ekonom** sets the gold answer for the BRF reference set: correct skuld/kvm band, avgift sustainability, new-build context, and especially the **cited soft signals** (does the citation actually support the flag?) — and the unit/denominator and new-build-context cases.
- A **bank mortgage advisor (bolånerådgivare)** sanity-checks the debt/avgift risk framing against how a lender reads belåningsgrad / höjningsrisk.
- The **experienced private buyer (recent bostadsrätt purchaser)** is the routine stand-in and the **filler/boilerplate** labeler ("useful and specific, or generic?") — the closest available calibration source when specialist time is scarce; also the default product-owner proxy.
- Labels stored keyed by `analysisId` in `evals/report-fixtures/labels.json`. The LLM-judge dimensions (verdict line, generic filler, synthesis quality, claim-uses-its-citation, soft-signal-support) are **calibrated against these human labels to ≥0.7 correlation before being trusted in CI** (ai-evals Verify-phase gate).

## 6. Guardrails

> Guardrail-vs-flywheel test: "if this behaviour goes wrong, is it catastrophic for the product's trust posture and legal line?" For Phase 4 the catastrophic cases are narrow and sharp: **(1) a buy/don't-buy verdict reaching the user** (crosses the "ej finansiell rådgivning" line, FM2/D-04) and **(2) a fabricated number or flag** (FM1/FM3/D-06). The strongest defence for both is **architectural, not a runtime check**: `reportSchema` has no `verdict`/`recommendation`/free-form-flag field, so a verdict is *unrepresentable* and a model-minted flag has nowhere to live (the same trick Phase 2 used to keep `betyg` out of the extraction schema). The online guardrails below are the cheap deterministic backstop layered after the single Sonnet call — they add negligible latency. Everything subjective (filler, synthesis nuance, tone) is a **flywheel**, not an online gate, because a sub-optimal-but-honest report is not catastrophic and an LLM judge on the request path would add cost + latency for a non-catastrophic signal.

### Online (Real-Time — every report, in `generate-report.ts` post-synthesis; cheap, deterministic)

| Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **Schema / no-verdict gate** | `messages.parse` Zod validation fails, OR the parsed `AiReport` contains any verdict/recommendation/buy-signal/free-form-flag key (a dev/CI invariant — the schema makes this unrepresentable, the guard fails loudly if a prompt/schema regression ever leaks one) | **Block** — never persist or render an invalid/verdict-shaped report. On parse failure: retry once (`max_tokens` only, §4b), then surface a Swedish "kunde inte generera rapporten" affordance. The verdict-key assertion is a hard CI invariant that fails the build. |
| **Refusal / truncation gate** | `stop_reason === "refusal"` (no retry) or `"max_tokens"` (retry once then give up); empty `parsed_output` | **Block + Flag** — throw the coded error (`CLAUDE_REFUSAL` / `CLAUDE_MAX_TOKENS` / `CLAUDE_PARSE_EMPTY`), surface a user message at the action layer; never present partial JSON as a real report. |
| **Fabricated-data / unknown-flag gate** | Any `citedClaim.sourceRef` does NOT resolve to a key present in the fact sheet, OR any `prioritizedFlagIds` entry is not in the fact sheet's pre-computed flag set (model-minted reference, FM1/FM3) | **Block** — a report that cites a datum or flag the input never contained is fabrication by definition; do not persist. Deterministic set-membership check against the closed fact sheet, sub-millisecond. (This is the runtime expression of the §5 "no fabricated data point" dimension.) |
| **Partial-data consistency gate** | A `themedSection.status` is `bedömd` while the fact sheet marked that source `ej_tillgänglig` (or the inverse) — i.e. the report claims to assess a source that was absent (FM4) | **Block** — a section asserting content for a missing source is silent fabrication-by-absence; force it to `ej_tillgänglig` / fail the persist. Deterministic check of section status vs. input source-presence flags. |
| **Per-report cost cap** | Computed SEK cost from `message.usage` via `costSek` approaching/exceeding the inherited **5 SEK** budget (e.g. an oversized fact sheet, or repeated retries with no cache hit) | **Block further spend** — abort extra retries, record the breach (`console.warn("[report-cost]", { analysisId, sek })`, §4 step 4), never loop the model on one report past budget. |
| **Soft-signal citation gate** (rides the existing Phase 2 post-extraction guardrails) | A non-null soft signal (`stambytePlanerat` etc.) with `sourceQuote === null`, or a quote not found verbatim on the cited `pageRef` | **Flag** — mark the signal low-confidence / "kontrollera källan"; an uncited soft signal must never feed a flag as if verified (D-11). Reuses the Phase 2 missing-citation guardrail unchanged. |

> **Deliberately NOT an online guardrail:** generic-filler detection, lead-synthesis quality, and the opinionated-but-no-verdict *tone* (vs. the verdict *shape*, which the schema gates). These need an LLM judge, which on the request path costs money and latency for a **non-catastrophic** quality signal — an honest, specific-but-bland report does no harm. They run as flywheel metrics. The one verdict failure that *is* catchable cheaply (a verdict-shaped key) is gated above; a verdict smuggled into free prose is caught by the flywheel LLM judge + human sampling, not a real-time gate.

### Offline (Flywheel — sampled batch, feeds prompt/threshold refinement)

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Opinionated-but-no-verdict adherence** (LLM judge) | Calibrated verdict-line judge over the nightly reference set + a weekly human (köparmäklare) spot-check of ~5 production reports; in production, sample reports a user **regenerated** (D-08) or rated as a strong signal | Any verdict/valuation slipping past the schema gate into prose → tighten the D-04 prohibition + few-shot in `REPORT_SYNTHESIS_PROMPT`, bump version, re-run. If the judge drifts from human labels, recalibrate. |
| **Generic-filler / specificity rate** (LLM judge) | Same nightly run; the "would this sentence be true of a different listing?" judge, calibrated against the experienced-buyer stand-in | Filler rate rising → strengthen the D-06 "cite a specific datum" instruction and the bad-example few-shot; add the offending fact sheet to the reference set. |
| **Cross-source synthesis quality** (LLM judge + human) | Nightly rubric judge on `leadSynthesis` against the §1b dimensions + buyer's-agent face-validity on the price-vs-finances and new-build cases | Synthesis reading as two disconnected facts, or mis-applying the debt band by building age → revise the synthesis prompt's cross-source + new-build-context guidance. |
| **Soft-signal accuracy & citation fidelity** | Re-run the labelled Phase 2 reference set nightly + on every `BRF_EXTRACTION_PROMPT_VERSION` bump; in production, sample reports where a soft-signal flag was shown | Missed/invented stambyte or unsupported citation rate rising → tighten the soft-signal extraction prompt, add the PDF to the reference set, re-run the full Phase 2 eval. |
| **Per-report cost & cache-hit distribution** | Continuous, from persisted `usage`/`sek` per report (the §7 trace) | p95 cost creeping toward 5 SEK, or `cache_read_input_tokens` staying 0 across regenerates → investigate prompt-cache invalidation (timestamp/unsorted key, §4b.4) and fact-sheet size. |
| **Regenerate rate per report** | Continuous production signal — a regenerate after a fresh report (not driven by D-08 staleness) is a soft "the report wasn't useful/right" signal | A cluster of immediate regenerates on a fact-sheet class → inspect those reports for filler/verdict/synthesis misses; feed into the reference set. |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** **Self-instrumented traces persisted to Supabase, surfaced via the existing PostHog — same right-sized posture as Phase 2 §7. No external LLM-observability platform this phase.** The volume is low (login-gated, manual-trigger, premium), each report is two bounded calls, and a solo dev on <$100/mo has no tracing infra to justify standing up Arize Phoenix / Langfuse. The minimum that actually drives decisions is already half-built: the `costSek` path in `cost.ts`. Per report, persist alongside the `analyses` row (or a `report_traces` jsonb): `analysisId`, synthesis `model` id + `REPORT_SYNTHESIS_PROMPT_VERSION` (and the soft-signal `BRF_EXTRACTION_PROMPT_VERSION`), `usage` (input/output/`cache_creation`/`cache_read` tokens), computed `sek` cost, `stop_reason`, latency, which online guardrails fired, the `dataFingerprint` (D-08 staleness), and whether the report was later regenerated.
> **GDPR / log hygiene (§1b, §3):** never persist the fact-sheet contents (financials) or the report prose in traces or logs — only `{ analysisId, code }` server-side and the token/cost/flag/version metadata above. The report itself lives in the RLS-scoped `analyses` jsonb; traces inherit that RLS.
> **Promote to Langfuse (self-hosted) only if** a future phase makes synthesis genuinely multi-step (plan→draft→critique) or adds more Claude calls worth tracing end-to-end. Explicitly out of scope for Phase 4 v1.

**Key Metrics to Track:**
1. **Per-report Sonnet cost (SEK) + cache-hit rate** — the §4b/<5 SEK budget guarantee; track the distribution (mean + p95), not just mean; watch `cache_read_input_tokens > 0` on regenerates.
2. **Regenerate rate** (and the split between D-08-staleness-driven vs. user-initiated-fresh) — the best production proxy for "the report wasn't useful/right"; broken down by fact-sheet class (strong / high-debt / new-build / partial).
3. **Partial-report rate** — share of reports with ≥1 `ej_tillgänglig` section (no BRF / no market data); confirms partial-data honesty is firing and tracks how often users generate before all sources are in.
4. **Guardrail-trip rate** — fabricated-data/unknown-flag gate, no-verdict-key invariant, partial-data consistency gate, refusal/truncation, cost cap. Any non-zero rate on the fabrication or verdict gates is a prompt/schema regression signal.
5. **Synthesis latency (p50/p95)** — feeds the "Genererar rapport…" spinner UX (§4b async) and detects oversized-fact-sheet slowdowns.

**Alert Thresholds:**
- **Any** report with computed cost ≥ 5 SEK → alert immediately (hard budget breach, §1 cost guard / FM-cost).
- p95 per-report cost > 3 SEK (75% of budget) → warning; investigate fact-sheet size and prompt-cache invalidation.
- Cache-hit rate on regenerates < 80% (or `cache_read_input_tokens` == 0 across a regenerate) → warning; a timestamp/unsorted key is silently invalidating the prefix (§4b.4).
- **Any** trip of the **fabricated-data/unknown-flag gate** or the **no-verdict-key invariant** in production → page; both should be impossible given the schema + set-membership guards, so a trip means a guardrail bypass or regression (FM1/FM2/FM3).
- Refusal / truncation / manual-fallback rate > 10% over a rolling window → investigate prompt/model/fact-sheet regression.
- Immediate-regenerate rate (fresh report regenerated within minutes, not staleness-driven) > 15% on any fact-sheet class → investigate that class for filler/verdict/synthesis misses.

**Smart Sampling Strategy:**
Weight human review (köparmäklare / BRF-ekonom / experienced-buyer stand-in, §1b roles) toward concerning signals rather than uniform sampling:
- **Every report that tripped the fabricated-data, unknown-flag, or partial-data-consistency gate** — review all; these are the FM1/FM3/FM4 catastrophic-adjacent cases.
- **Reports a user regenerated immediately** (not staleness-driven) — the strongest "this wasn't right/useful" signal; check for verdict-in-prose, filler, or a mis-read cross-source thread.
- **New-build and high-debt-old-building reports** — the cases most likely to mis-apply the debt band by building age (domain FM2); sample for the buyer's-agent/bolånerådgivare face-validity check.
- **Price-premium-vs-weak-finances reports** — verify the lead synthesis connects the sources and stays short of valuation (domain FM3, D-05).
- **Partial-data reports** — confirm the gap is stated honestly, not papered over (FM4).
- A small **uniform random baseline** (~1–2 reports/week) so the sampler doesn't only ever see flagged cases — guards against signal-metric divergence (ai-evals). Reviewed misses feed straight back into the §5 reference dataset (the flywheel).
