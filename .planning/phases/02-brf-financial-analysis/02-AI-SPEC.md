## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Extraction (structured data extraction from PDFs)

**Description:**
A logged-in user uploads a BRF årsredovisning (Swedish housing-cooperative annual report) PDF and the system extracts a fixed set of financial figures — skuld/kvm (debt per square metre), avgiftsnivå (fee level), kassaflöde (cash flow), and underhållsplan status (maintenance-plan status) — each with a per-field confidence score, the verbatim source quote, and a page reference. Claude performs extraction ONLY; deterministic TypeScript code computes the transparent A–F grade from the extracted numbers (locked decision D-08). "Good" means: every returned figure is either correct or visibly flagged as low-confidence for the user to verify/correct, never silently wrong; total Claude cost stays under 5 SEK per analysis; and the same PDF always yields the same extraction-then-grade result.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
<!-- The 3-5 behaviors that absolutely cannot go wrong in this system -->
1. **Confident hallucination of a financial figure.** A fabricated or misread number (e.g. skuld/kvm off by 10x) presented as high-confidence corrupts the A–F grade and destroys user trust. Mitigation: per-field confidence, mandatory source-quote + page citation, sanity-range validation (2,000–15,000 SEK skuld/kvm for Stockholm).
2. **Letting the model grade.** If Claude ever produces the A–F score (or anything resembling a judgement), the grade becomes non-deterministic and non-auditable, breaking the public "Så räknar vi" methodology promise (D-08, D-09). Extraction output must contain numbers only — no scoring fields.
3. **Silent low-quality extraction on scanned PDFs.** A scanned/poor-OCR document that returns plausible-but-wrong numbers without elevated uncertainty (D-14). Low confidence must be surfaced, not buried.
4. **Schema/normalization drift.** Returning a shape the deterministic scorer cannot consume (missing field, wrong unit, string where number expected) silently drops a metric from the grade. Mitigation: Zod-validated structured output + null-tolerant normalization layer mirroring `src/lib/schemas/listing.ts`.
5. **Cost blowout.** Re-reading a large (up to ~20 MB) PDF on every retry or progress poll, or using Sonnet for extraction, breaches the <5 SEK/analysis budget. Mitigation: Haiku for extraction, prompt caching of the document block, bounded retries.

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** Swedish residential real estate / housing-cooperative (BRF) financial due diligence — consumer-facing fintech.

**User Population:** Prospective home buyers in Sweden evaluating a specific apartment (bostadsrätt) before bidding. Mostly financial non-experts who cannot read an årsredovisning unaided — the exact gap the product fills ("the analysis their mäklare won't provide"). They are anxious, time-pressured (bidding rounds move in days), and making the largest purchase of their lives. The "domain experts" whose judgement the system must approximate are: **BRF-ekonomer / förvaltningskonsulter** (HSB, Riksbyggen, SBC, Fastum), **bank mortgage analysts** (SBAB, SEB, Handelsbanken all publish BRF-nyckeltal guides), **mäklare**, and **auktoriserade revisorer / redovisningskonsulter** who prepare and audit the reports under BFN rules.

**Stakes Level:** High. Not life-safety (so not Critical), but a wrong financial signal can steer a multi-million-SEK purchase decision, and Swedish consumer-protection norms treat misleading financial information seriously. The product's entire trust proposition (D-08/D-09/D-10/D-11) exists precisely because the stakes are high enough that "silently wrong" is unacceptable.

**Output Consequence:** A buyer reads the A–F grade and per-metric breakdown and uses it to decide whether to bid, how high to bid, or to walk away. A falsely good grade (under-reported debt, missed negative cash flow) can lead a buyer into a förening facing a large avgiftshöjning or special assessment after purchase — a direct financial loss and a reputational/liability risk for the product. A falsely bad grade can make a buyer abandon a sound home. Because every figure is editable (D-12) and source-cited (D-11), the realistic worst case is a *confident, plausible, uncorrected* error — which is why confidence calibration, not just accuracy, is the load-bearing property.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

These are the dimensions a BRF-ekonom or bank analyst actually applies when reading an årsredovisning. They map directly onto the four extracted fields plus the trust mechanics.

```
Dimension: Skuldsättning per kvm (debt per square metre, bostadsrättsyta)
Good: The figure is the räntebärande skuld divided by upplåten bostadsrättsyta — the BFNAR 2023:1 definition — read from the mandatory nyckeltal table or computed from balansräkningen + ytuppgift, in SEK/m². Falls in a plausible Stockholm band (~2,000–15,000 SEK/m²; <5,000 strong, >12,000 a red flag).
Bad: Total debt reported as debt/kvm (off by the apartment count), debt divided by total area instead of bostadsrättsyta (two distinct mandated figures — the wrong one understates risk), or a value outside the sanity band presented at high confidence.
Stakes: Critical
Source: BFNAR 2023:1 mandatory nyckeltal; SBAB/SEB/Handelsbanken bank guides (<5,000 good / >12,000 red flag).
```
```
Dimension: Årsavgift per kvm (annual fee level, SEK/m²/year)
Good: Årsavgift per kvm upplåten med bostadsrätt — the BFNAR 2023:1 metric — distinguished from monthly avgift, from avgift including/excluding heating/broadband, and from hyresintäkter (rental income from commercial premises). Read in context: a low fee is only good if the förening still saves enough for maintenance.
Bad: Monthly fee reported as annual (12x error), the listing's apartment-specific avgift confused with the föreningen-level per-kvm figure, or a "low fee = good" reading that ignores under-maintenance.
Stakes: High
Source: BFNAR 2023:1; practitioner consensus (Boio, SBC, Fastighetsförmedling) that a low avgift at the cost of underhåll is a hidden risk.
```
```
Dimension: Kassaflöde / sparande per kvm (operating cash flow and saving capacity)
Good: Cash flow from löpande verksamhet, read with the standard expert correction — avskrivningar are non-cash, so the meaningful question is whether the förening generates positive cash after interest and real costs. "Sparande per kvm" (saving available for future underhåll) is the long-term-health metric: experts cite ≥200–300 SEK/m² as healthy, <120–200 as a warning.
Bad: Reporting bokföringsmässigt resultat (which includes non-cash avskrivningar) as if it were cash position, or missing that a large negative result is mostly avskrivningar (benign) vs. a genuine cash deficit (serious). Treating a one-off (e.g. fönsterbyte) as a recurring deficit.
Stakes: High
Source: SBAB/SEB nyckeltal guides; HSB/Riksbyggen sparande-per-kvm thresholds; the "räkna bort avskrivningar" expert rule of thumb.
```
```
Dimension: Underhållsplan status (maintenance plan currency and coverage)
Good: Correctly states whether an underhållsplan exists, its horizon, and whether large items (stambyte, tak, fasad, fönster, hiss) are planned/funded. Distinguishes "finns och är aktuell" from "finns men inaktuell/gammal" from "saknas/nämns ej" — and flags planned major works that imply future avgiftshöjning.
Bad: Reading "förvaltningsberättelsen nämner underhåll" as "an actual plan exists", or missing a disclosed upcoming stambyte/large investment that is the single biggest cost-shock risk to the buyer.
Stakes: High
Source: Practitioner guides (Boio, ERF, SBC) — planned major maintenance and its funding is a primary buyer red flag (avgiftshöjning, special assessment).
```
```
Dimension: Source traceability & honest uncertainty (the trust contract)
Good: Every figure shows the verbatim quote + page it came from (D-11), and any figure that is inferred, scanned-OCR-derived, or out of sanity range is visibly flagged "Osäker — kontrollera själv" (D-10). A domain expert can verify each number against the PDF in seconds.
Bad: A confident number with no citation, a citation that points to the wrong figure/page, or a low-quality scan extraction presented without elevated uncertainty. Silent wrongness is the cardinal sin.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Phase decisions D-10/D-11/D-12; consumer-protection expectation that financial claims be sourced and correctable.
```

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

Domain-specific manifestations — beyond generic "hallucination":

1. **Unit / denominator confusion.** Swedish årsredovisningar report several debt and area figures (total skuld vs räntebärande skuld; total yta vs upplåten bostadsrättsyta; monthly vs annual avgift). Picking the wrong denominator produces a number that *looks* plausible and passes schema validation but understates or overstates risk by a large factor. This is the most likely high-impact error and the reason for sanity-range bands.
2. **Avskrivningar misread as a cash deficit.** A förening can show a large negative bokföringsmässigt resultat that is almost entirely non-cash avskrivningar (especially post-2014 after BFN banned progressiv avskrivning, which pushed many föreningar to linjär and produced "scary" book losses). Treating book result as cash health is the classic novice misread the product must avoid.
3. **Layout diversity across 50+ accounting-firm templates.** K2 vs K3 reports, and dozens of förvaltningsbolag layouts (HSB, Riksbyggen, SBC, Fastum, boutique firms), place the same nyckeltal in different tables, footnotes, or the förvaltningsberättelse. Pre-2023 reports may lack the now-mandatory nyckeltal table entirely, forcing inference. Extraction that only works on the common template silently fails on the long tail.
4. **Stale / partial maintenance signal.** "Underhållsplan finns" stated years ago, or a planned stambyte buried in flowing text rather than a table, is easy to miss — yet it is the dominant predictor of a future avgiftshöjning, the exact cost shock buyers fear most.
5. **Scanned-PDF over-confidence.** OCR of a scanned older report misreads digits (e.g. 1 vs 7, lost decimals/thousands separators) and the model reports the misread number at normal confidence (D-14). The thousands-separator / decimal-comma convention in Swedish (1 234,56) is a specific OCR hazard.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

- **BFNAR 2023:1 + BFN vägledning (mandatory BRF nyckeltal, from FY2023).** Defines the now-required nyckeltal and their exact formulas — årsavgift/kvm (bostadsrättsyta), skuldsättning/kvm (räntebärande skuld ÷ upplåten bostadsrättsyta), räntekänslighet (räntebärande skuld ÷ årsavgiftsintäkter), energikostnad/kvm, and more. The system's extraction definitions and methodology page should align to these formulas so figures are comparable to what banks and ekonomer use. Pre-FY2023 reports will not contain this table — handle as inference + lower confidence.
- **K2 (BFNAR 2016:10) vs K3 (BFNAR 2012:1) accounting frameworks.** Most BRFs are "mindre företag" and may file under either; K3 affects component depreciation and how avskrivningar/underhåll appear. The extractor must be robust to both presentations. (BFN also banned progressiv avskrivning for BRF — relevant to interpreting historical results.)
- **GDPR.** Uploaded årsredovisningar are public documents but can contain personal data (styrelse names, sometimes member-level figures), and uploads are tied to a logged-in user (D-05). Constraints: store PDFs in a private RLS-protected Supabase bucket (no public URLs), never log raw PDF bytes or extracted financials in plaintext logs (only a content hash + token/cost usage, per §4b), and apply the project's existing retention/RLS posture. Lawful basis is the user's own request to analyse a document they supplied.
- **Consumer-protection / no-financial-advice posture.** The product presents a transparent, rule-based assessment (D-08/D-09), not personalised financial advice — keep the "Så räknar vi" methodology public and frame output as decision-support the user verifies (D-11/D-12), not a recommendation to buy. This is a framing constraint, not a licensing one (the product is explicitly out of scope for valuation/price prediction per REQUIREMENTS Out-of-Scope).

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Direct Anthropic TypeScript SDK (`@anthropic-ai/sdk`) + Zod v4 for structured extraction

**Version:** `@anthropic-ai/sdk@^0.102.0` (latest stable, June 2026) + `zod@^4.3.6` (already installed; import via `zod/v4`)

**Rationale:**
For a single, bounded structured-extraction call against one PDF, the direct vendor SDK is the lowest-overhead correct choice. Two facts make it especially strong here: (1) the SDK ships native **structured outputs** — `client.messages.parse({ output_config: { format: zodOutputFormat(schema) } })` validates Claude's JSON against a Zod schema server-side and returns a typed `parsed_output`, eliminating hand-rolled tool-use plumbing; and (2) it has first-class **PDF document support** (`DocumentBlockParam` with a base64 PDF source plus document-level **citations**), which directly satisfies the source-quote + page-reference trust requirement (D-11). The team is TypeScript-only (Next.js 16), Claude is the chosen model, Zod v4 is already the project's external-data validation standard, and the extraction is one deterministic step — so an orchestration framework (LangChain, agent SDKs) would add abstraction with no payoff. Anthropic SDK + Zod gives the smallest amount of code, fully typed, with built-in citations and caching for cost control.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| LangChain (TypeScript) `withStructuredOutput` + Zod | Adds abstraction overhead for a single bounded extraction call; revisit only if extraction becomes multi-step or needs provider-swapping across many PDF layouts |
| Claude Agent SDK | Built for autonomous tool-use agent loops (filesystem/shell, MCP, hooks) — unused weight for one structured extraction call |
| CrewAI / Haystack / AutoGen / Google ADK | Python-only (or wrong vendor) — project is TypeScript-only (Next.js 16) |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Partial — accepted consciously. The Messages API call shape, `output_config`/`zodOutputFormat`, document blocks, and citations are Anthropic-specific. The mitigation is architectural, not framework-level: the call is isolated behind a single `extractBrfFinancials()` module and the **Zod schema is the contract**, so swapping providers means re-implementing one function, not rewriting the app. The downstream deterministic scorer and the normalization layer are provider-agnostic. Trade-off is worth it: native citations + structured outputs are the two features that most directly de-risk the critical failure modes (hallucination, missing source), and re-building them on a model-agnostic framework would be net more code.

### Core Imports
```typescript
import Anthropic from "@anthropic-ai/sdk";
import { zodOutputFormat } from "@anthropic-ai/sdk/helpers/zod"; // Zod -> JSON schema + validated parse
import { z } from "zod/v4";                                      // project convention (see listing.ts)
```

### Entry Point Pattern
```typescript
// src/lib/brf/extract.ts — runs in a Next.js server action / route handler ONLY
const client = new Anthropic(); // reads ANTHROPIC_API_KEY from env

// Numbers + confidence + source ONLY. No grade field — code grades (D-08).
const BrfExtraction = z.object({
  skuldPerKvm: z.object({
    value: z.number().nullable(),       // SEK/m²; null when not found
    confidence: z.number().min(0).max(1),
    sourceQuote: z.string().nullable(), // verbatim text from the PDF (D-11)
    pageRef: z.number().int().nullable(),
  }),
  // ...avgiftsniva, kassaflode, underhallsplanStatus (full schema in §4b.1)
});

export async function extractBrfFinancials(pdfBase64: string) {
  const message = await client.messages.parse({
    model: "claude-haiku-4-5",   // Haiku for extraction (Sonnet reserved for Phase 3+ synthesis)
    max_tokens: 2048,
    temperature: 0,              // deterministic-as-possible extraction
    system: BRF_EXTRACTION_SYSTEM_PROMPT,
    messages: [{
      role: "user",
      content: [
        {
          type: "document",
          source: { type: "base64", media_type: "application/pdf", data: pdfBase64 },
          citations: { enabled: true },      // ground every figure in the PDF (D-11)
          cache_control: { type: "ephemeral" }, // cache the doc across retries (cost, §4b.5)
        },
        { type: "text", text: "Extrahera nyckeltalen enligt schemat. Lämna fält null om de inte finns." },
      ],
    }],
    output_config: { format: zodOutputFormat(BrfExtraction) },
  });

  if (message.stop_reason === "refusal") throw new Error("Claude refused extraction");
  return message.parsed_output; // typed as z.infer<typeof BrfExtraction>, already Zod-validated
}
```

### Key Abstractions
| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| `client.messages.parse()` | Variant of `messages.create()` that takes an `output_config` format and returns a validated `parsed_output`. | The single extraction call — preferred over `create()` because it does schema validation for you. |
| `zodOutputFormat(schema)` | Helper that converts a Zod v4 schema to the JSON-schema `output_config.format` and wires up parsing. | Whenever the response must match a Zod schema (your case). Alternative: `jsonSchemaOutputFormat()` for raw JSON schema. |
| `DocumentBlockParam` (`type: "document"`) | A content block carrying a PDF (base64/URL/text) with optional `citations`, `cache_control`, `title`, `context`. | Sending the uploaded årsredovisning PDF; base64 source for Supabase-Storage bytes. |
| `citations: { enabled: true }` + response `citations` | Document-level citation config; Claude returns char-range citations tying output text back to the PDF. | To populate the source-quote + page-reference trust UI (D-11). |
| `cache_control: { type: "ephemeral" }` | Marks a content block as a prompt-cache breakpoint (5-minute default TTL). | On the large PDF block so retries / re-grades don't re-bill the full document (§4b.5). |
| `message.stop_reason` (`"end_turn" \| "max_tokens" \| "refusal" \| ...`) + `message.usage` | Why generation stopped + token accounting (incl. `cache_read_input_tokens`). | Detect refusals/truncation; compute per-analysis cost against the <5 SEK budget. |

### Common Pitfalls
1. **Calling the SDK from the browser.** `new Anthropic()` in a client component leaks the API key. Instantiate only inside server actions / route handlers (the SDK throws unless `dangerouslyAllowBrowser` is set — do NOT set it). All BRF extraction is server-side (D-05 login-gated).
2. **Letting `parse()` lull you into trusting the *value*.** `messages.parse()` guarantees the JSON matches the *shape*, not that the numbers are *correct*. A hallucinated `skuldPerKvm` still passes schema validation. You must layer domain sanity-checks (range bands) and the confidence field on top — schema validity ≠ extraction correctness.
3. **Required vs. nullable fields.** Structured outputs emit `additionalProperties: false` and treat all properties as required. If a figure is genuinely absent from the PDF, the model is still forced to emit the key — so model every field as `.nullable()` (value can be `null`), never `.optional()` (Anthropic structured outputs do not honour optional the way you expect). Mirror the null-tolerant style in `listing.ts`.
4. **`max_tokens` too low truncates the JSON.** If the schema is large and `max_tokens` is hit mid-object you get `stop_reason: "max_tokens"` and a parse failure, not a clean error. Set `max_tokens` generously (≥2048 here) and explicitly check `stop_reason`.
5. **Re-sending the PDF without caching = cost blowout.** A 20 MB scanned PDF tokenizes into a large input; D-12 corrections and D-13 progress retries can re-run extraction. Without `cache_control` on the document block, each run re-bills the whole PDF and blows the <5 SEK budget (§4b.5). Also: PDF support is on Messages models but watch per-request size/page limits — verify against current limits in docs for very large scans.
6. **Temperature drift breaks determinism expectations.** Even at `temperature: 0` extraction is not bit-for-bit reproducible. D-08's determinism guarantee lives in the *scorer*, not the extractor — never represent the extraction step as deterministic to the user.

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**
- **Extraction model:** `claude-haiku-4-5` — cheap, fast, and accurate enough for bounded number-pulling. Sonnet is reserved for Phase 3+ synthesis and is overkill (and over-budget) for extraction.
- **`temperature: 0`** — minimise drift; extraction wants the single most-likely reading, not creativity.
- **`max_tokens: 2048`** — comfortably above the JSON output size (4 metrics × {value, confidence, quote, page}); prevents mid-object truncation.
- **`output_config.format: zodOutputFormat(BrfExtraction)`** — server-side schema-constrained decoding + validated parse.
- **`citations: { enabled: true }`** on the document block — required for the source-quote/page trust UI (D-11).
- **`cache_control: { type: "ephemeral" }`** on the document block — cost control across retries/re-grades (§4b.5).
- Pass a `metadata.user_id` (hashed) for abuse tracing; keep all calls server-side (D-05).

**Core Pattern:**
The canonical shape is **single-shot constrained extraction** inside a server action, then **handoff to deterministic code**:
```
analyze-brf.ts (server action)
  1. Auth gate (login-only, D-05) + fetch PDF bytes from Supabase Storage
  2. base64-encode -> extractBrfFinancials(pdfBase64)        // the ONE Claude call
  3. normalizeBrfExtraction(parsed_output)                    // null-tolerant, mirrors listing.ts
  4. applySanityChecks() -> downgrade confidence on out-of-range values (e.g. skuld/kvm)
  5. computeBrfGrade(normalized)                              // deterministic A–F, NO Claude (D-08)
  6. persist {extraction, grade, usage/cost} to analyses row + emit progress (D-13)
```
The Claude step returns only numbers + confidence + citations; steps 3–5 are pure TypeScript so the grade is reproducible and auditable for the public "Så räknar vi" page (D-09). Low-confidence or out-of-range fields flow straight to the "Osäker — kontrollera själv" UI (D-10) and the inline-edit path (D-12), which re-runs steps 4–5 (NOT step 2) on correction.

**Tool Use:**
No agentic/function-calling tools are needed — this is pure structured extraction, so `output_config` (not `tools`) is the mechanism. The "tools" here are integrations, not Claude tools:
- **Supabase Storage** — new PDF bucket + RLS; read bytes server-side, base64-encode for the document block. (No public URLs; keep PDFs private.)
- **`@anthropic-ai/sdk`** — the single dependency added this phase. If the SDK has dynamic requires that trip Next.js bundling, add it to `serverExternalPackages` in `next.config.ts` (apify-client precedent).
- **Citations response parsing** — map returned char-range citations to `{ sourceQuote, pageRef }` per field for the trust UI.

**State Management:**
Stateless per call — no conversation history, no agent memory. Durable state lives in Supabase:
- The uploaded PDF in Storage (one per analysis, replaceable — D-06).
- Extraction result + computed grade + token/cost usage persisted on the `analyses` row (new jsonb column or related table; RLS must cover it).
- Progress state for the live step UI (D-13): polling or Supabase Realtime — extraction runs server-side and survives the user leaving the page. The `parsed_output` + `usage` are the only things worth persisting from the model; never persist raw prompt/PDF in logs (PII/financial data).

**Context Window Strategy:**
A single årsredovisning (typically 10–40 pages) fits comfortably in Claude's context, so no chunking/RAG is needed for the common case. Strategy:
- **Whole-document, single pass** — send the full PDF as one document block; let Claude attend across pages to find each figure.
- **Cache the document block** so retries and the (out-of-scope-here but adjacent) synthesis phase don't re-pay for the same bytes.
- **Scanned/very large PDFs (up to ~20 MB, D-14):** if a document exceeds per-request page/size limits, fall back to splitting by page range and merging extractions field-by-field (keep the highest-confidence non-null per field). Flag scanned docs up front and lean harder on confidence (D-14). Verify current PDF page/size limits against docs before locking the threshold.

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice.

> **Note (TypeScript project):** this project uses **Zod v4** (`import { z } from "zod/v4"`) instead of Pydantic. The patterns below are the TypeScript/Zod equivalents.

### Structured Outputs with Zod (Pydantic equivalent)

The Zod schema is the contract between Claude and the deterministic scorer. Define it once; the SDK constrains decoding and validates the parse.

```typescript
// src/lib/schemas/brf.ts
import { z } from "zod/v4";

// One reusable shape per extracted figure: value + confidence + citation.
// .describe() text is emitted into the JSON schema and steers extraction.
const extractedField = <T extends z.ZodTypeAny>(value: T) =>
  z.object({
    value: value.nullable().describe("Null if the figure is not present in the document"),
    confidence: z.number().min(0).max(1).describe("Model's confidence in this value, 0–1"),
    sourceQuote: z.string().nullable().describe("Verbatim text from the PDF this value came from"),
    pageRef: z.number().int().positive().nullable().describe("1-based page number of the source quote"),
  });

export const brfExtractionSchema = z.object({
  skuldPerKvm: extractedField(z.number()).describe("Skuld per kvm bostadsyta, SEK/m²"),
  avgiftsniva: extractedField(z.number()).describe("Årsavgift per kvm bostadsyta, SEK/m²/år"),
  kassaflode: extractedField(z.number()).describe("Kassaflöde från löpande verksamhet, SEK"),
  underhallsplanStatus: extractedField(
    z.enum(["finns_aktuell", "finns_inaktuell", "saknas", "oklart"]),
  ).describe("Status för underhållsplan"),
  // NO grade / score / rating field — code grades (D-08).
});

export type BrfExtraction = z.infer<typeof brfExtractionSchema>;
```
**How the SDK uses it:** pass `zodOutputFormat(brfExtractionSchema)` to `messages.parse({ output_config: { format } })`. The SDK converts the Zod schema to JSON schema (`z.toJSONSchema()` under the hood), constrains the model's output, and returns a Zod-validated `message.parsed_output` typed as `BrfExtraction`.

**Retry logic:**
- **Validation/parse failure** (rare with constrained outputs, but possible on `max_tokens` truncation or `refusal`): retry **once** with the same inputs. On a second failure, surface a user-facing "Vi kunde inte läsa dokumentet automatiskt — fyll i manuellt" and route to the inline-edit path (D-12). Do NOT silently return partial data.
- **Transient API errors** (429/5xx): the SDK retries with backoff by default; cap with `maxRetries: 2` on the client and a hard timeout.
- **Log on failure:** `stop_reason`, `usage`, schema-validation error path, a content hash of the PDF (never the PDF/financials themselves) — enough to reproduce without storing PII.
- **Never retry on `stop_reason: "refusal"`** — surface immediately; retrying wastes budget.

### Async-First Design

- **Everything is async/Promise-based.** `messages.parse()` returns a `Promise`; call it with `await` inside the server action. There is no separate async entry point to forget (unlike Python's `asyncio.run()`), but the equivalent mistake is **blocking the request thread on a long extraction**: a 20 MB scanned PDF can take many seconds. Run extraction in a background server task (D-13) and let the client poll / subscribe to Supabase Realtime; do not hold the HTTP response open for the whole extraction.
- **`await`, do not stream, for this use case.** Streaming (`stream: true` / `messages.stream()`) is for token-by-token UX. Structured extraction must be fully received before Zod validation, so **await the complete `parsed_output`**. Stream only the *progress steps* (D-13 "Läser… → Extraherar… → Beräknar…") via your own status updates, not the model tokens.
- **Parallelism:** if a large PDF is split by page range (§4.Context), run the per-range extractions with `Promise.all()` and merge field-by-field.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

- **System vs. user separation:** put the *role, rules, and schema discipline* in the `system` prompt (Swedish-document context, "extract only — never grade", "use null when absent", confidence calibration guidance). Put the *task instruction* in the user turn alongside the document block. Keep the system prompt in a versioned `src/lib/brf/prompt.ts` so prompt changes are reviewable and tied to eval runs.
- **Few-shot:** prefer **inline** few-shot — 1–2 short worked examples of a tricky figure (e.g. distinguishing total debt from debt/kvm, or spotting "ingen underhållsplan") embedded in the system prompt. No dynamic retrieval is warranted (single bounded task, no corpus).
- **Token budget:** set `max_tokens` **explicitly (2048)** — never leave it to default in production. Keep the system prompt tight; the PDF dominates input tokens, so verbosity in the prompt is cheap but the document block is not (hence caching).
- **Calibrate confidence in the prompt:** explicitly instruct lower confidence for scanned/ambiguous text and when a figure is inferred rather than stated verbatim — this is what makes D-10's "Osäker" flagging meaningful.

### Context Window Management

This is extraction, not RAG or a long conversation, so the management strategy is **document sizing**, not chunking or summarisation:
- **Default:** whole PDF in one document block (årsredovisningar fit comfortably).
- **Cache** the document block (`cache_control: ephemeral`) so retries and adjacent phases reuse it.
- **Oversized/scanned fallback:** split by page range, extract per range, merge by taking the highest-confidence non-null value per field. Never silently drop a field — a missing figure becomes `value: null` with `confidence: 0`, which the scorer treats as "not assessable" and the UI flags.
- No conversation summarisation and no agent compaction apply here.

### Cost and Latency Budget

- **Budget:** < 5 SEK per analysis (≈ 0.45 USD at ~11 SEK/USD).
- **Per-call estimate (Haiku, extraction):** a typical 20-page digital årsredovisning is on the order of low-tens-of-thousands of input tokens plus ~1k output tokens. At Haiku pricing this lands well under 1 SEK per first extraction — comfortably inside budget with headroom for a retry. **Verify current Haiku per-token prices against the pricing page before locking the budget assertion.**
- **Caching:**
  - **Prompt caching** of the PDF document block (`cache_control: ephemeral`, ~5-min TTL): a retry or a re-grade within the window reads the cached document at a fraction of the input cost (`usage.cache_read_input_tokens`).
  - **Exact-match result cache:** key on the PDF content hash → if the same bytes are re-uploaded (D-06 replace with identical file), return the stored extraction without calling Claude at all.
  - Semantic caching is not applicable (binary PDFs, not text queries).
- **Model routing:** Haiku for extraction (this phase); Sonnet reserved strictly for Phase 3+ synthesis. Never route extraction to Sonnet — it triples cost for no accuracy gain on number-pulling.
- **Cost tracking:** read `message.usage` after every call (input, output, `cache_creation_input_tokens`, `cache_read_input_tokens`), compute SEK cost, and persist it on the analysis row to monitor the budget in production (§7).

## 5. Evaluation Strategy

> The system is **structured extraction**, so the dominant eval concerns are **field accuracy, denominator/unit correctness, confidence calibration, source-citation fidelity, and grade determinism** — not generative quality. The load-bearing property (per §1b Output Consequence) is **calibration**: a wrong figure is tolerable if flagged low-confidence; a wrong figure presented at high confidence with a wrong/absent citation is the cardinal sin. Evals therefore weight calibration and citation fidelity above raw accuracy.
>
> Evals split cleanly into two layers that match the architecture:
> - **Extractor evals** (non-deterministic, Claude) — run against the labelled reference dataset; the unit of truth is the per-field `{value, confidence, sourceQuote, pageRef}`.
> - **Scorer evals** (deterministic, pure TS, D-08) — ordinary unit tests; given known extraction input, the A–F grade is asserted exactly. These are property/golden tests, not "AI evals".

### Dimensions

| Dimension | Rubric (Pass/Fail) | Measurement | Priority |
|-----------|--------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Schema & normalization validity** (§1 FM4) | PASS: `messages.parse` returns Zod-valid `parsed_output`; every field present (value-or-`null`), units are SEK/m² (skuld, avgift), SEK (kassaflöde), enum (underhållsplan); `normalizeBrfExtraction()` produces a shape `computeBrfGrade()` consumes without throwing. FAIL: parse/validation error reaches the user, a field is silently dropped, or a string/wrong-unit value passes into the scorer. | **Code** — Zod parse + `normalizeBrfExtraction` + scorer contract assertion. Cheap, runs on every reference example and in CI. | **Critical** |
| **Denominator / unit correctness** (§1b FM1) | PASS: skuld/kvm = räntebärande skuld ÷ upplåten bostadsrättsyta (BFNAR 2023:1), not total debt and not ÷ total yta; avgift is annual SEK/m² (not the monthly or apartment-specific figure); kassaflöde is from löpande verksamhet. Extracted value matches the domain-expert label within ±5% (rounding/page-table tolerance). FAIL: off by ~apartment-count (total debt), ~12x (monthly→annual), or wrong-area denominator — i.e. plausible-but-wrong. | **Code** (exact/±5% match vs labelled value) **+ Human** (BRF-ekonom adjudicates which mandated figure is correct when the report has several). | **Critical** |
| **Sanity-range adherence** (§1 FM1) | PASS: any out-of-band figure (skuld/kvm outside ~2,000–15,000 SEK/m² Stockholm; avgift outside ~300–1,200 SEK/m²/år) is automatically confidence-downgraded by `applySanityChecks()` and surfaced as "Osäker", OR is a genuine outlier with a correct citation. FAIL: an out-of-band value passes through at normal confidence and silently feeds the grade. | **Code** — deterministic range check; assert downgrade fired. | **Critical** |
| **Confidence calibration** (§1b "honest uncertainty", FM3/FM5) | PASS: across the reference set, fields the expert marked wrong/ambiguous/scanned carry confidence below the "Osäker" threshold, and fields marked clearly-stated carry high confidence — i.e. low-confidence flags correlate with actual errors (target: ≥80% of mislabelled fields flagged; Brier/ECE tracked as the headline calibration metric). FAIL: a wrong figure on a scanned PDF reported at high confidence (silent wrongness), or everything flagged "Osäker" (uninformative). | **Code** (confidence vs error correlation over the dataset) **+ Human** (decide the per-field "Osäker" threshold with a BRF-ekonom). | **Critical** |
| **Source-citation fidelity** (§1b trust contract, D-11) | PASS: every non-null figure has a `sourceQuote` that appears verbatim in the cited `pageRef`, and the quote actually contains/supports the number. A reviewer can verify the figure against the PDF in seconds. FAIL: confident number with no citation, citation pointing to the wrong page/figure, or a paraphrased quote that doesn't appear in the PDF. | **Code** (substring + page-bound check: quote present on `pageRef`) **+ LLM judge** (does the quote *support* the value? — calibrated against human, target ≥0.7 corr) **+ Human** sampling. | **Critical** |
| **Maintenance-plan classification** (§1b FM4-maint) | PASS: `underhallsplanStatus` correctly distinguishes `finns_aktuell` / `finns_inaktuell` / `saknas` / `oklart`, and a disclosed upcoming major work (stambyte, tak, fasad, hiss) is captured rather than read as "plan exists". FAIL: "förvaltningsberättelsen nämner underhåll" graded as an actual current plan, or a buried planned stambyte missed. | **LLM judge** (classification vs labelled status; calibrated) **+ Human** for the buried-text edge cases. | **High** |
| **Avskrivningar-vs-cash interpretation** (§1b FM2) | PASS: a large negative *bokföringsmässigt resultat* that is mostly non-cash avskrivningar is NOT extracted as a cash deficit; kassaflöde reflects cash from löpande verksamhet. FAIL: book result reported as cash position. | **Human** (revisor/redovisningskonsult adjudicates) on the subset of reports with large negative results. | **High** |
| **Grade determinism** (§1 FM2, D-08) | PASS: `computeBrfGrade(sameNormalizedInput)` always returns the identical A–F grade and per-metric breakdown; extraction output contains NO grade/score field; golden inputs map to expected grades. FAIL: any non-determinism in the scorer, or a scoring field appearing in the extraction schema. | **Code** — pure-function unit/golden tests (Vitest), independent of Claude. | **Critical** |
| **Cost adherence** (§1 FM5, §4b budget) | PASS: per-analysis Claude cost < 5 SEK computed from `message.usage`; retries read the cached document (`cache_read_input_tokens` > 0); extraction uses Haiku, never Sonnet. FAIL: any analysis ≥ 5 SEK, or a model other than `claude-haiku-4-5`. | **Code** — assert computed SEK cost and model id over the reference run. | **High** |
| **Refusal / truncation handling** (§4b retry) | PASS: `stop_reason: "refusal"` surfaces the manual-entry path (D-12) without retry; `max_tokens` truncation retries once then falls back to manual entry — never returns partial silent data. FAIL: refusal retried (wasted budget) or truncated JSON surfaced as a real result. | **Code** — simulated stop-reason fixtures. | **Medium** |

### Eval Tooling

**Primary Tool:** **Vitest** (extractor + scorer eval harness) **+ Promptfoo** (prompt regression in CI). Code-based validators carry the bulk of the work, per the ai-evals guidance for structured extraction.

> **Why not Phoenix / RAGAS:** the default Phoenix + RAGAS stack is Python and RAG-oriented; this is a TypeScript, single-shot extraction project with a Zod contract and a deterministic scorer. RAG metrics (faithfulness/context-precision) do not apply — there is no retrieval. Self-instrumented tracing into Supabase (§7) is the right-sized observability for a solo-dev, cost-conscious build with no existing platform. Adopt Langfuse later only if multi-phase tracing (extraction → Phase 3/4 synthesis) outgrows table-based traces.

**Setup:**
```bash
npm i -D vitest @vitest/coverage-v8 promptfoo
# Eval data lives in the repo; PDFs in a private fixtures dir (gitignored if PII-sensitive),
# labels in a checked-in JSON manifest keyed by PDF content hash.
#   evals/
#   ├── fixtures/                 # 12–20 real årsredovisning PDFs (gitignored; synced from private storage)
#   ├── labels.json               # expert-labelled {hash: {skuldPerKvm, avgiftsniva, ...}} ground truth
#   ├── extractor.eval.ts         # runs extractBrfFinancials over fixtures, scores all §5 dimensions
#   ├── scorer.test.ts            # deterministic golden tests for computeBrfGrade (no Claude)
#   ├── citation-judge.ts         # LLM-judge: does sourceQuote support value? (calibrated)
#   └── promptfooconfig.yaml      # prompt regression: assert on a frozen 4–6 PDF subset
# Scorer tests need NO ANTHROPIC_API_KEY; extractor evals are gated behind RUN_LLM_EVALS=1 to avoid spend.
```

**CI/CD Integration:**
```bash
# Always-on (free, deterministic) — blocks merge:
npx vitest run evals/scorer.test.ts src/lib/brf            # grade determinism + normalization contract

# Prompt regression on the frozen subset (cheap, ~4–6 Haiku calls) — runs on prompt.ts changes:
npx promptfoo eval -c evals/promptfooconfig.yaml --no-cache=false

# Full extractor eval (cost-gated, manual/nightly — not per-commit):
RUN_LLM_EVALS=1 npx vitest run evals/extractor.eval.ts     # accuracy + calibration + citation over full set
```
Gate the full LLM eval behind a label/env flag so PR CI stays free and fast; run it nightly and before any `prompt.ts` or model change.

### Reference Dataset

**Size:** **12–20 real BRF årsredovisningar** to start (per ai-evals "10–20 high-quality examples"). Begin at 12, grow toward 20 as production failures surface; expand from real misses, not hypothetical coverage.

**Composition** (deliberately spans the §1b failure surface):
- **Critical-path happy cases (~4):** post-FY2023 digital reports with the mandatory BFNAR 2023:1 nyckeltal table — figures read directly, high confidence expected.
- **Template diversity (~4):** different förvaltningsbolag layouts (HSB, Riksbyggen, SBC, Fastum, boutique) and **K2 vs K3** presentations — the long-tail layout risk (FM3).
- **Inference-required (~2):** **pre-2023** reports lacking the nyckeltal table — figures must be computed from balansräkning + yta, expected lower confidence.
- **Scanned / OCR-hazard (~2):** older scanned PDFs with thousands-separator / decimal-comma traps (FM5) — must trigger the scanned heads-up (D-14) and elevated uncertainty.
- **Avskrivningar trap (~2):** reports with a large negative bokföringsmässigt resultat that is mostly non-cash (FM2) — must not be read as a cash deficit.
- **Maintenance edge (~2):** one with a disclosed upcoming stambyte buried in flowing text; one where "underhåll" is merely mentioned but no current plan exists (FM4-maint).
- **Adversarial / denominator traps:** at least 2 reports that present *both* total-debt and debt/kvm, or monthly *and* annual avgift, where picking the wrong figure looks plausible (FM1).
- **Out-of-sanity outlier (~1):** a genuinely high skuld/kvm (>12,000) to confirm the range check downgrades-or-passes-with-citation correctly rather than blindly clipping.

**Labeling:** **Domain-expert ground truth, not engineer-guessed.** A **BRF-ekonom / förvaltningskonsult** (§1b roles) labels the correct per-field value, the correct denominator, the expected "Osäker" flag, and the expected source page/quote for each PDF; an **auktoriserad redovisningskonsult/revisor** adjudicates K2/K3 and avskrivningar-vs-cash edge cases. The **product owner (solo dev)** owns the manifest and acts as default proxy where external experts are unavailable, signing off the "Så räknar vi" thresholds (D-09). Labels are stored keyed by **PDF content hash** so the manifest is reproducible and never stores raw financials in logs (§4b/GDPR). LLM-judge dimensions (citation support, maintenance classification) are **calibrated against these human labels** to ≥0.7 correlation before being trusted in CI.

## 6. Guardrails

> Guardrail-vs-flywheel test: "would this going wrong be catastrophic for the product's trust proposition?" For this product, **silent wrongness** (a confident, plausible, uncorrected error) is the catastrophic case (§1b), so the online guardrails center on *forcing uncertainty to the surface* rather than blocking output. They are cheap deterministic checks layered after the single Claude call — they add negligible latency.

### Online (Real-Time — every analysis, in the `analyze-brf.ts` server action, post-extraction)

| Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **Schema/normalization gate** | `messages.parse` Zod validation fails, or `normalizeBrfExtraction` cannot produce a scorer-consumable shape | **Block** — never feed an invalid shape to the scorer. Retry once (§4b); on second failure route to manual entry (D-12) with "Vi kunde inte läsa dokumentet automatiskt". |
| **Refusal / truncation gate** | `stop_reason === "refusal"` (no retry) or `"max_tokens"` (retry once) | **Block + Flag** — surface manual-entry path; never present partial JSON as a result. |
| **Sanity-range downgrade** | Extracted value outside the BFNAR/bank bands (skuld/kvm ∉ ~2,000–15,000; avgift ∉ ~300–1,200 SEK/m²/år; negative where impossible) | **Flag** — force confidence below the "Osäker" threshold regardless of model confidence; render "Osäker — kontrollera själv" (D-10). Value still shown (editable, D-12) — do not silently drop. |
| **Missing-citation flag** | Non-null `value` with `sourceQuote === null`, or quote not found verbatim on the cited `pageRef` | **Flag** — mark the figure low-confidence and "kontrollera källan"; a number with no verifiable source must never read as trustworthy (D-11). |
| **Low-confidence flag** | Per-field `confidence` below the calibrated "Osäker" threshold (incl. all fields when the PDF is detected as scanned, D-14) | **Flag** — visible "Osäker" badge + route into the inline-edit affordance (D-12). |
| **Scanned-PDF heads-up** | Document detected as scanned/image-only before/at extraction | **Flag** — show the D-14 notice ("Skannad PDF — utläsningen kan bli osäkrare") and apply a stricter confidence threshold. |
| **Per-analysis cost cap** | Computed SEK cost from `message.usage` approaching/exceeding 5 SEK (e.g. oversized scan re-billing without cache hit) | **Block further spend** — abort extra retries, record the breach, surface manual entry; never loop the model on one document past budget. |
| **No-scoring-field assertion** | Extraction `parsed_output` contains any grade/score/rating-like key | **Block** (dev/CI invariant) — the model must never grade (D-08); fail loudly so a prompt/schema regression can't leak a model-produced grade into production. |

### Offline (Flywheel — sampled batch, feeds prompt/threshold refinement)

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Field accuracy & denominator correctness** | Re-run the labelled reference set nightly + on every `prompt.ts`/model change; in production, sample analyses where the user **edited** a field (D-12) — an edit is a strong "model was wrong here" signal | Below per-field accuracy floor → inspect failing layouts, add the PDF to the reference set, revise prompt few-shots (esp. denominator disambiguation). |
| **Confidence calibration (Brier/ECE)** | Same nightly reference run; in production, compare flagged-vs-edited and unflagged-vs-edited rates | Mis-calibration (users editing high-confidence fields, or "Osäker" on correct fields) → re-tune the confidence prompt guidance and "Osäker" threshold with the BRF-ekonom. |
| **Citation fidelity** | Calibrated LLM-judge over the nightly set + a weekly human spot-check of ~5 production analyses | Quote-doesn't-support-value rate rising → tighten citation prompt; if judge drifts from human, recalibrate. |
| **User-edit rate per field** | Continuous production signal (which field gets corrected most, on which template/K2-K3/scanned class) | A field/template with a high edit rate is an emerging failure mode → add representative PDFs to the reference set and address in the prompt. |
| **Cost & cache-hit distribution** | Continuous, from persisted `usage` per analysis | p95 cost creeping toward 5 SEK or cache-hit rate dropping → investigate PDF sizes, caching window, retry behaviour. |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** **Self-instrumented traces persisted to Supabase** (a `brf_extraction_traces` row or jsonb on the `analyses` row), surfaced via the project's existing **PostHog** for product analytics. No external LLM-observability platform this phase — the volume is low (login-gated, premium), the call is single-shot, and Supabase + PostHog already exist. Persist per analysis: PDF **content hash** (not bytes), model id, `usage` (input/output/`cache_creation`/`cache_read` tokens), computed SEK cost, `stop_reason`, latency, which guardrails fired, per-field final confidence, and whether each field was later user-edited.
> **GDPR (§1b):** never log raw PDF bytes, the extracted financials, or the source quotes in plaintext traces — only the content hash + token/cost/flag metadata. PDFs stay in the private RLS bucket; traces inherit the analyses-row RLS.
> Promote to **Langfuse (self-hosted)** when Phase 3/4 synthesis adds multi-step Claude calls worth tracing end-to-end.

**Key Metrics to Track:**
1. **Per-analysis Claude cost (SEK)** and **cache-hit rate** — the §4b budget guarantee; track distribution (mean + p95), not just mean.
2. **User-edit rate per field** (D-12 corrections) — the single best production proxy for extraction accuracy; broken down by template / K2-K3 / digital-vs-scanned.
3. **"Osäker"-flag rate per field** and its agreement with edits — live confidence-calibration signal (flagged-and-edited = good; unflagged-and-edited = silent-wrongness escaping; flagged-and-unedited-correct = over-flagging).
4. **Extraction failure / manual-fallback rate** — refusals, truncations, schema failures routing to manual entry (D-12).
5. **Extraction latency (p50/p95)** — feeds the D-13 progress UX and detects oversized-scan slowdowns.

**Alert Thresholds:**
- **Any** analysis with computed cost ≥ 5 SEK → alert immediately (hard budget breach, §1 FM5).
- p95 per-analysis cost > 3 SEK (75% of budget) → warning, investigate caching/PDF sizes.
- Cache-hit rate on retries < 80% → warning (caching/window misconfigured).
- Manual-fallback rate > 15% over a rolling window → investigate prompt/model/layout regression.
- **Unflagged-field edit rate > 10%** (users correcting fields the system showed as confident) → high-priority alert: silent wrongness is leaking past calibration; review immediately with a domain expert.
- Any non-null figure shipped with a missing/unverifiable citation in production (should be impossible given the online guardrail) → page; indicates a guardrail bypass.

**Smart Sampling Strategy:**
Weight human review (BRF-ekonom / mäklare face-validity, §1b roles) toward concerning signals rather than uniform sampling:
- **Every analysis where a user edited a high-confidence (unflagged) field** — the strongest "the model was confidently wrong" signal; review all of these.
- **Out-of-sanity-range** values that were surfaced (verify the downgrade and citation were correct).
- **Scanned PDFs** and **pre-2023 / unusual-template** reports (the long-tail layouts most likely to fail silently, FM3/FM5).
- **Refusals and truncations** (understand why the model declined a real document).
- A small **uniform random baseline** (~1–2 analyses/week) so the sampler doesn't only ever see flagged cases (guards against signal-metric divergence). Reviewed misses feed straight back into the reference dataset (§5 flywheel).
